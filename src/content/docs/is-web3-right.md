# Is Web3 Right for Your Community?

*Guide to is web3 right for your community?*

---

## How to evaluate whether Web3 tools are the right fit for your community's needs.

## The Honest Question
Web3 isn't for everyone. Some communities thrive with spreadsheets, group chats, and bank accounts. Adding blockchain doesn't automatically make things better.

## When Web3 Adds Value
- You need transparent, verifiable financial management
- You want programmable governance (automatic execution of decisions)
- You're coordinating across borders or jurisdictions
- You want to connect to the broader Web3 ecosystem (funding, tools, community)
- You value immutable records of decisions and transactions

## When Web3 Doesn't Add Value
- Your group is small (under 10) and high-trust
- Financial activity is minimal
- Members aren't tech-comfortable and don't want to learn
- A spreadsheet does everything you need
- You're adding complexity for its own sake

## Questions to Ask
1. What problem would Web3 solve that our current tools can't?
2. Are our members willing to learn new tools?
3. Do we have at least 2-3 people who can support others technically?
4. Is the added complexity justified by the benefits?
5. Could we start with a simpler tool and upgrade later?

## The Hybrid Approach
Most communities benefit from a hybrid: use Web3 where it adds clear value (treasury management, governance voting) and traditional tools where it doesn't (communication, scheduling, document management).

## Moving Forward
Be honest about whether Web3 serves your community or your curiosity. Both are valid — but don't confuse them.
