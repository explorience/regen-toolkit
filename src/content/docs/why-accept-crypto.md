# Why Accept Crypto Donations?

Your organization already accepts bank transfers and mobile money. Maybe you take cash at events. But there is a growing group of donors who want to give in a way that fits their values, and right now your organization might be invisible to them. Cryptocurrency donations could open a meaningful new stream of support, and getting set up is simpler than you might expect.

Here is the core argument. A significant and growing number of people hold cryptocurrency as a major part of their wealth. Many of them actively want to give it away. If your organization makes it easy, you become visible to donors who are already looking for organizations like yours.

## The Tax Advantage

When someone donates cryptocurrency directly to your organization, they can often avoid capital gains tax entirely. This makes donating crypto potentially more valuable to the donor than donating cash.

Here is a practical example. A donor bought Ethereum worth $1,000 several years ago. It is now worth $20,000. If they sell it, they owe tax on the $19,000 gain. If they donate it directly to your nonprofit, they skip the tax bill and receive a federal income tax deduction for the full $20,000 value. The math often works out better for the donor, which means more money reaches your mission.

Crypto donations through donor-advised funds have grown rapidly. The pattern is consistent: once donors understand the tax benefit, they actively look for organizations that accept crypto. If yours is among them, you benefit.

## Lower Processing Costs

Every organization knows how processing fees eat into donations. Credit card companies typically take 2 to 3 percent. Payment platforms charge their own share. For organizations depending on smaller donations, this adds up.

Cryptocurrency transfers can reduce these costs. There is no credit card company in the middle. For cross-border donations in particular, the difference is significant. International bank wires can take days and cost $25 to $50 each. Crypto transfers from anywhere in the world arrive in minutes or seconds with minimal fees.

This is especially relevant for organizations in East Africa that receive support from the diaspora or from international donors. Faster, cheaper donations mean more of each contribution reaches your programs.

## Access to a Different Donor Community

Crypto donors tend to be younger on average and often hold progressive views on technology and social change. Many are explicitly interested in funding projects aligned with their values, including regenerative work, climate solutions, and community-led development.

The Pineapple Fund, a Bitcoin-only donor, gave $55 million to 60 nonprofits back in 2018. That was early days. The crypto wealthy have grown substantially since then, and so has their appetite for meaningful giving.

When your organization accepts crypto, you signal that you understand this world and are comfortable operating in it. That matters to these donors. They want to support organizations that feel like peers, not like institutions that barely understand what cryptocurrency is.

## Quadratic Funding: Amplifying Small Donations

One of the most interesting developments in crypto philanthropy is quadratic funding. Rather than large donors having disproportionate influence, this model amplifies small donations by matching them based on how many people give, not how much each person gives.

Gitcoin Grants has distributed over $60 million through quadratic funding rounds, with hundreds of thousands of unique donors contributing to thousands of projects. Regenerative and open-source projects have been significant beneficiaries.

Your organization does not need to understand the technical mechanics to participate. You need to show up where these conversations are happening. Many regenerative and community-focused projects on Gitcoin are doing exactly that.

## Addressing the Concerns Honestly

Here is where honesty matters. Volatility is real. If you receive a donation in Ether and the price drops the next day, the value of that donation has fallen. The straightforward solution is to convert donations to cash immediately through a platform that handles this for you.

The platforms listed below do exactly this. Donors send crypto. Your organization receives fiat currency. Neither of you needs to hold cryptocurrency long-term to benefit from the donation.

Security also needs attention. If your organization does hold crypto directly, use multi-signature wallets that require multiple people to approve transactions. Never share private keys. Establish clear internal policies before you start. The good news is that reputable platforms handle most of this complexity for you.

Some cryptocurrencies, particularly those using proof-of-work systems, have significant energy footprints. If environmental responsibility is central to your mission, you can choose which tokens you accept. Many modern networks, including Ethereum after its 2022 upgrade, use proof-of-stake and consume a tiny fraction of the energy of older proof-of-work systems.

## Getting Started

You do not need to become a crypto expert. These platforms handle the complexity:

- **The Giving Block** is widely used and integrates with donor management software. Best for organizations expecting significant crypto volume.
- **Endaoment** is nonprofit-focused and handles compliance and reporting. Good if you want a hands-off approach.
- **Every.org** offers a free and simple option. Best if you are starting out or have a smaller operation.
- **Givepact** is a simpler option designed for smaller organizations that want to accept crypto without a complex setup.

Most platforms let donors send crypto while your organization receives fiat currency. The donor gets the tax benefit. You get the funds. Neither of you needs to manage cryptocurrency volatility.

## Try This

Start small and learn as you go:

1. **Check your donor base.** Do you have supporters who are tech-forward, work in the digital space, or are younger? Crypto giving often resonates with these communities, including the East African diaspora.
2. **Choose one platform.** Every.org is free and takes 15 minutes to set up. Start there rather than overthinking the choice.
3. **Update your donate page.** Add a single line mentioning you accept cryptocurrency. You do not need to explain it further.
4. **Thank crypto donors specifically.** When someone gives in crypto, acknowledge it in a way that shows you understand what they did. These donors often feel like early adopters and remember who made them feel welcome.
