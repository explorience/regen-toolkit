# Proposal Writing for DAOs

*Guide to proposal writing for daos*

---

## How to write proposals that pass and actually get implemented.

## Why Proposals Fail
Most rejected proposals fail not because the idea is bad but because the proposal is unclear, poorly scoped, or doesn't address community concerns.

## Anatomy of a Good Proposal

### 1. Title
Clear, specific, descriptive. "Fund Community Garden Phase 1" not "Garden Stuff."

### 2. Summary (2-3 sentences)
What are you proposing? What will it cost? What will it achieve?

### 3. Background/Context
Why is this needed? What problem does it solve? What evidence supports this?

### 4. Specification
Exactly what will be done. Deliverables, timeline, responsible parties.

### 5. Budget
Detailed breakdown. How much, for what, paid to whom.

### 6. Success Criteria
How will we know this worked? Measurable outcomes.

### 7. Risks
What could go wrong? How will you mitigate?

## Before You Propose

### Temperature Check
Before writing a formal proposal, discuss informally. Get feedback. Gauge support.

### Build Coalition
Talk to potential supporters. Address concerns before the vote, not during.

### Address Objections Preemptively
If you know someone will object, address their concern in the proposal itself.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Too vague**: "We'll improve community engagement" — how?
2. **No budget**: Proposals without numbers can't be evaluated
3. **No success criteria**: How will we know if this worked?
4. **No timeline**: When will each deliverable be completed?
5. **Surprise proposals**: Springing proposals without prior discussion

## Moving Forward
Draft your proposal. Share it informally. Iterate based on feedback. Then submit formally.
