# Social Tokens for Creators

*Guide to social tokens for creators*

---

## How creators and communities can use social tokens for engagement and value exchange.

## What Are Social Tokens?
Social tokens are community-specific tokens that represent membership, access, or reputation within a community. Unlike utility tokens, their primary value comes from the social context they exist in.

## Types of Social Tokens

### Creator Tokens
Tokens issued by individual creators to their audience. Holders get access to exclusive content, events, or governance over the creator's direction.

### Community Tokens
Tokens representing membership in a community. Can be earned through contribution or purchased. Used for governance, access, and identity.

### Reputation Tokens
Non-transferable tokens that represent contribution history. Think of them as a resume on-chain.

## Use Cases for Local Nodes
- **Event access**: Token-gated events for active members
- **Governance weight**: More contribution = more voice
- **Incentive alignment**: Reward contributors with tokens
- **Community identity**: Visual proof of membership (NFTs, badges)

## Pitfalls
1. Don't financialize community relationships unnecessarily
2. Tokens should reflect value, not create it artificially
3. Avoid speculation — design for utility, not trading
4. Regulatory considerations (securities law)

## Moving Forward
Consider social tokens when you need a way to track and reward contribution. Don't use them just because you can.
