# Governance Attacks

*Understanding and preventing attacks on community governance*

---

## What Are Governance Attacks

Governance attacks happen when someone gains control of a DAO's voting power to benefit themselves at the expense of the community.

---

## Types

### Token Accumulation
- Buying up voting tokens
- Gaining majority control

### Sybil Attacks
- Creating fake identities
- Manipulating votes

### Flash Loans
- Borrowing tokens temporarily
- Voting, then returning

---

## Real Examples

### Steem Incident
Justin Sun acquired 30% of tokens to gain control [1].

### Beanstalk Attack
Flash loan used to pass malicious proposal, stealing $182M [2].

---

## Prevention

### Quorum Requirements
- Minimum participation needed
- Prevents small groups controlling

### Time Locks
- Delay between vote and execution
- Allows challenge period

### Reputation Systems
- Weight votes by history
- Makes sybil harder

### Token Vesting
- Tokens unlock over time
- Prevents sudden accumulation
