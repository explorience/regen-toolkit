# Building Internal Capacity

*Guide to building internal capacity*

---

## How to develop the skills and systems your community needs to sustain Web3 operations.

## What Is Internal Capacity?
Internal capacity is your community's ability to do things independently — without relying on external experts, consultants, or hired help. It's the skills, knowledge, and systems that live within your members.

## Key Capacity Areas

### Technical
- Wallet management
- Smart contract interaction
- Treasury operations
- Security practices

### Governance
- Facilitation
- Proposal writing
- Conflict resolution
- Decision-making processes

### Communication
- Writing and storytelling
- Social media management
- Community management
- Outreach and partnerships

### Financial
- Bookkeeping and accounting
- Grant writing
- Budget management
- Fundraising

## Building Capacity

### 1. Identify Gaps
What can your community do now? What can't you do? Where do you depend on external help?

### 2. Map Existing Skills
You'd be surprised what members already know. Survey your community.

### 3. Create Learning Pathways
For each gap, create a path: resources, training, mentorship, practice.

### 4. Practice with Low Stakes
Let people practice new skills on small tasks before critical ones.

### 5. Document Everything
Create guides, templates, and processes that anyone can follow.

## Moving Forward
Capacity building is slow but essential. Invest in your members' skills — it's the highest-return investment your node can make.
