# What Web3 Can and Can't Do

Web3 represents a fundamental shift in how we think about the internet—moving from platforms that own your data to systems where you own your digital life. But it's not a magic solution to every problem. Understanding both its strengths and limitations helps you engage meaningfully and set realistic expectations for your regen projects.

## What Web3 Can Do

### Own Your Digital Assets

Web3 lets you truly own digital items—cryptocurrency, NFTs, tokens representing real-world assets. Unlike buying a song on iTunes (where you're essentially licensing it), owning an NFT means you control it. You can sell it, gift it, or hold it—no middleman needed.

This ownership extends beyond just collectibles. You can own governance tokens that give you voting rights in DAOs, carbon credit tokens that represent environmental impact, or even fractional ownership of physical assets like real estate or art.

### Permissionless Innovation

Anyone can build on public blockchains. No app store approval, no platform taking 30% fees, no centralized authority saying "no." This enables **DeFi** (decentralized finance), **DAOs** (decentralized autonomous organizations), and entirely new economic models that were impossible in Web2.

Think about it: A developer in Nairobi can create a financial protocol that serves users in Buenos Aires without asking permission from any corporation. This opens doors for regen projects in regions where traditional financial systems are broken or exclusionary.

### Transparent Verification

Transactions on blockchains are publicly verifiable. This matters enormously for regen work:

- **Charity donations**: Donors can verify funds actually reach recipients
- **Supply chain tracking**: Prove products are ethically sourced and sustainably produced
- **Governance**: See exactly how votes were counted and decisions made
- **Impact measurement**: Track and verify environmental or social outcomes

This transparency builds trust in systems where trust is often lacking.

### Censorship Resistance

Once something is on a public blockchain, it's extremely difficult to remove. This protects free speech in authoritarian contexts and preserves digital history.

For regen movements, this means your community's records, funding flows, and impact data can't be erased by hostile governments or corporations. Your work persists.

## What Web3 Can't Do (Yet)

### Scale Like Traditional Systems

Bitcoin handles ~7 transactions per second; Visa handles ~24,000. Ethereum's base layer handles about 15-30 transactions per second. Blockchain tech is improving (Layer 2 solutions, new consensus mechanisms) but mass adoption faces real technical hurdles.

This matters for regen projects: You can't run a global streaming service or social media platform entirely on-chain yet. But you can run a community currency, governance system, or impact tracking platform.

### Replace All Databases

Most data doesn't need blockchain's permanence and cost. Your social media posts, emails, or Netflix preferences work fine in traditional databases—adding blockchain adds complexity without benefit.

The "blockchain trilemma" means you typically get to choose only two of three: decentralization, security, and scalability. For many use cases, traditional databases are the right tool.

### Solve All Trust Problems

Blockchains verify data *on-chain*—but what about the real world? If you tokenize a house, someone still needs to legally transfer ownership. If you track carbon credits, someone needs to verify the actual carbon reduction happened.

**Oracles** (services feeding real-world data to blockchains) help, but the "oracle problem" remains: How do you trust the bridge between physical reality and digital records? This is particularly important for regen projects measuring real-world impact.

### Guarantee Privacy

Public blockchains are... public. Your transactions are visible. While pseudonymous (not tied to your real name by default), sophisticated analysis can often link addresses to identities.

For regen projects dealing with sensitive data (refugee aid, medical records, endangered species locations), this presents real challenges. Zero-knowledge proofs and privacy-focused blockchains are evolving, but perfect privacy remains elusive.

## Practical Examples for Regen Builders

- **Can**: Send money globally without Western Union—borderless, 24/7, low fees
- **Can't**: Stream educational videos at scale (blockchain can't handle that volume yet)
- **Can**: Crowdfund a reforestation project and guarantee backers receive tokens if funded
- **Can't**: Store your community's private photos (on-chain storage is expensive and public)
- **Can**: Create a community currency for your local regen network
- **Can't**: Replace your entire CRM system with blockchain (yet)

## Making Smart Choices

The key is matching the tool to the job. Web3 excels at:

- **Value transfer**: Moving money, tokens, or digital assets
- **Governance**: Decentralized decision-making and voting
- **Verification**: Proving claims about impact, ownership, or authenticity
- **Coordination**: Organizing communities and resources without central control

But it's not ideal for:
- **Mass data storage**: Photos, videos, large datasets
- **Real-time applications**: Gaming, streaming, messaging
- **Privacy-sensitive operations**: Without careful design

## The Path Forward

Web3 isn't replacing the internet—it's adding new capabilities. The most successful regen projects will use Web3 where it shines (ownership, transparency, coordination) while relying on traditional systems where they work better (storage, speed, privacy).

As a regen builder, your job is to understand these trade-offs and make informed decisions. The technology is evolving rapidly, but the principles of good project design remain constant: solve real problems, serve your community, and use the right tools for the job.
