# Building in Public

*Guide to building in public*

---

## How to share your journey openly and attract community support.

## What Is Building in Public?
Building in public means sharing your progress, failures, and learnings openly as you build. For local nodes, this means posting updates, sharing decisions, and being transparent about challenges.

## Why It Works
- **Attracts supporters**: People want to join something that's actively growing
- **Creates accountability**: Public commitments are harder to abandon
- **Builds trust**: Transparency demonstrates integrity
- **Generates content**: Your journey IS the content
- **Helps others**: Other nodes learn from your experience

## What to Share

### Progress Updates
- What you built this week/month
- Metrics and milestones
- Photos from events

### Lessons Learned
- What went wrong and how you fixed it
- What you'd do differently
- Advice for others starting out

### Behind the Scenes
- Decision-making processes
- Financial transparency
- Team dynamics

## Where to Share
- Twitter/X (reach the Web3 community)
- Farcaster (reach the crypto-native community)
- Blog posts (for depth)
- Newsletter (for committed followers)
- Discord/Telegram (for community updates)

## Best Practices
1. Be consistent — weekly updates beat monthly dumps
2. Be honest — share failures alongside wins
3. Be specific — "We held our first event with 12 attendees" beats "Things are going great!"
4. Engage with responses — building in public is a conversation

## Moving Forward
Start sharing. Even small updates count. Consistency matters more than polish.
