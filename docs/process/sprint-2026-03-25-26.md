# Regen Toolkit Content Sprint - March 25-26, 2026

A record of how 67 articles were processed, structured, and deployed across a two-day sprint.

## Starting Point

**What existed:**
- 254 article stubs in Matt's taxonomy spreadsheet, organized across 12 domains
- ~21 articles already on a standalone Astro/Starlight site (`regen-toolkit-site`)
- A separate GitHub repo (`explorience/regen-toolkit`) with raw markdown content
- Matt's feedback on 4 specific articles (scams, seed phrases, wallet comparison, key terms)
- A Google Doc ("Civic Intelligence Infrastructure - Mapping Architecture") with 60+ source references

**The problem:** Content was scattered, most articles were stubs (<200 words), and there was no structured metadata connecting the pieces.

## Phase 1: Content Processing (Night of March 25)

### Inventory & Triage

All 254 articles were categorized by word count into three phases:
- **Phase 1:** 46 articles with 800+ words (ready for editorial pipeline)
- **Phase 2:** 43 articles with 200-799 words (need expansion)
- **Phase 3:** 139 stubs under 200 words (need writing from scratch)

### Editorial Pipeline

Each Phase 1 article went through a 5-stage AI editorial pipeline:

1. **Research** - Verify claims, check for outdated information, identify gaps
2. **Writing** - Expand thin sections, add practical examples, ensure audience-appropriate language
3. **Fact-checking** - Verify technical accuracy, confirm links work, flag unsupported claims
4. **Editing** - Style consistency, remove AI slop phrases, ensure active voice
5. **Critique** - Final quality gate for strategic alignment and ethical review

Articles were processed in batches of 5 using Claude Opus sub-agents. The gateway could only handle one sub-agent at a time, so batches ran sequentially.

### Style Standards

- **Voice:** Friendly, practical, active voice, "you" not "one"
- **Audience:** Young women and community leaders in Somaliland/East Africa
- **Word counts:** Foundations 800-1200, Applied 1500-2000, Playbooks 800-1500
- **Required:** "Try This" exercises, References with real links, jargon defined on first use
- **Banned:** AI slop phrases ("let's explore", "navigate", "journey", "landscape"), `[Source X]` markers, emoji in bodies, exclamation cheerleading

Full style guide: [`CONTRIBUTING-CONTENT.md`](../CONTRIBUTING-CONTENT.md)

### Output

46 articles processed to publication quality, with:
- Consistent frontmatter (title, description, section, track, status, sources)
- "Try This" practical exercises
- Human-readable References sections with real links
- No source markers or AI slop language

### Frontmatter Enrichment

All 67 published articles were enriched with structured metadata based on Matt's taxonomy:

**New fields added:**
- `tags.function` - What problem the article solves (10 categories: Education, Governance, Security, Community-Building, Coordination, Impact-Measurement, Economic-Design, Funding, Identity, Infrastructure)
- `tags.domain` - Primary knowledge domain (7 categories: Web3-Literacy, Community, Technical, Ecosystem, Governance, Personal, Economics)
- `tags.systems` - Cross-cutting systems concepts (12 options including Decentralization, Trust-Networks, Feedback-Loops, Commons, Resilience)
- `audience` - Target reader (Individual, Local-Organizer, Community-Steward, DAO-Operator, Researcher)
- `maturity` - Complexity level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced)
- `related` - 2-4 cross-links to related articles

**Distribution:**
- Maturity: 28 Beginner, 31 Intermediate, 8 Advanced
- Audience: 30 Individual, 14 Community-Steward, 13 Local-Organizer, 8 DAO-Operator, 2 Researcher

## Architecture Decisions

1. **Flat file structure** - Articles directly in `src/content/docs/` rather than nested subdirectories. Cleaner URLs, simpler navigation.
2. **Manual sidebar** - Configured by hand rather than auto-generated, allowing precise control over section ordering and grouping.
3. **Sequential processing** - Gateway limitations meant one sub-agent at a time. Batches of 5 articles kept each run manageable.
4. **Matt's taxonomy as metadata, not structure** - Tags enrich articles with Matt's framework without forcing every article into a template. "Structure follows content."
5. **No template tyranny** - Schema elements (value chains, systems concepts) are applied only when they add value to a specific article.
