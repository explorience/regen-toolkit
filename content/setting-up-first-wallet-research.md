# Setting Up Your First Wallet

## Overview

A cryptocurrency wallet is your gateway to Web3—it stores your private keys and allows you to interact with blockchains. Unlike a traditional bank account, you fully control your funds, but also bear full responsibility for security.

## Key Concepts

### Private Keys vs Public Keys
Your wallet generates a **private key** (a secret password that controls your funds) and a **public key** (like an email address, shareable with others). From the private key, derive a **public address**—a string of characters where others can send you crypto. Never share your private key!

### Hot vs Cold Wallets
- **Hot wallets** connect to the internet (MetaMask, Rainbow, Coinbase Wallet). Convenient for frequent use but more vulnerable to hacking.
- **Cold wallets** stay offline (Ledger, Trezor hardware wallets). Best for storing larger amounts securely.

### Seed Phrases
Most wallets give you a 12 or 24-word **seed phrase** (recovery phrase) when you set up. This phrase can recreate your wallet on any device. Write it down—paper or metal backup—and store it somewhere safe. Never store it digitally or share it.

## Practical Examples for Maya

1. **Installing MetaMask**: Download the browser extension or mobile app. Create a password, then write down your seed phrase. This is your first wallet—great for trying dApps.

2. **Getting testnet ETH**: Use faucets (like faucet.sepolia.org) to get free test ETH on testnet networks. Practice sending transactions without real money.

3. **Hardware wallet for savings**: If you hold more than ~$500 in crypto, consider a Ledger. It requires physical button presses to confirm transactions—much harder for hackers to steal.

## Security Best Practices

- **Never share your seed phrase**—no legitimate service will ask for it
- **Use hardware wallets** for significant amounts
- **Enable wallet-specific passwords** beyond device passwords
- **Verify website URLs** before connecting your wallet (phishing sites are common)
- **Start small**—practice with small amounts before larger transactions
