## Key Concepts

### What Is a Crypto Wallet?
- A crypto wallet is software that lets you send, receive, and store cryptocurrency. [Source D]
- Unlike a traditional bank, there's no middleman—you have full control of your funds. [Source D]
- Think of it like a digital combination of a wallet (for carrying money) and a keys ring (for accessing accounts). [Source S]

### Hot Wallets vs. Cold Wallets
- **Hot wallets** connect to the internet (MetaMask, Rainbow, Coinbase Wallet). Good for small amounts and frequent use. [Source D]
- **Cold wallets** stay offline (Ledger, Trezor hardware wallets). Best for larger amounts you don't need to access daily. [Source D]
- For beginners starting out, a hot wallet like MetaMask is the recommended first step. [Source S]

### Private Keys and Seed Phrases
- A **private key** is like the PIN to your bank card—it proves you own the funds. NEVER share it. [Source D]
- Most wallets give you a 12 or 24-word **seed phrase** (also called "recovery phrase") when you set up. [Source S]
- This seed phrase can recreate your wallet on ANY device. If you lose it, you lose access to your funds—forever. [Source S]
- The seed phrase is mathematically impossible to hack. Even supercomputers couldn't guess it in thousands of years. [Source D]

### Wallet Address
- Your **wallet address** is a long string of letters and numbers (like `0x7a250...`) that represents your account. [Source D]
- This is safe to share with others so they can send you crypto—it's like giving someone your email address. [Source D]

## Step-by-Step Setup Process (MetaMask)

Based on the sources, here's the recommended setup flow:

1. **Download MetaMask** - Go to metamask.io/download and select Chrome/Brave browser or iOS/Android app. [Source S]
2. **Click "Get Started"** - Opens the setup wizard. [Source S]
3. **Click "Create a Wallet"** - Select this if it's your first time. [Source S]
4. **Create a password** - This password only works on the specific device you create it on. [Source S]
5. **Write down seed phrase** - MetaMask will show you 12 words. Write them down on paper. [Source S]
6. **Confirm seed phrase** - MetaMask asks you to select the words in order to verify you wrote them down correctly. [Source S]
7. **Configure preferences** - You can click "No Thanks" to opt out of data collection. [Source S]
8. **Access Account 1** - Your receiving address is ready to receive ETH or ERC-20 tokens. [Source S]

## Examples & Analogies for Maya

### Wallet = Digital Bank Account
Just like Maya keeps physical money in a leather wallet, MetaMask is a digital wallet that holds digital money (cryptocurrency). [Source S]

### Seed Phrase = Master Key / Genetic Code
The 12-word seed phrase is like the master key to your financial kingdom. If you lose it, you lose access to everything—no key, no money. [Source S]
Analogy: The name "seed phrase" is like genetic code. Lose it, and you lose the plant—permanently. No backup, no recovery.

### Hardware Wallet = Safe
If you have a lot of crypto, a hardware wallet (like Ledger or Trezor) is like putting your valuable items in a safe—it stores your crypto offline, making it much harder for hackers to steal. [Source D]

### Gas Fees = Postage
Every transaction on Ethereum needs a small fee called "gas," paid in ETH. Like postage—you need a tiny bit of ETH to send anything. Without ETH in your wallet, you can't send, swap, or trade. [Source S]

## Security Best Practices

From Bankless Academy [Source D]:
1. Store your recovery phrase securely in a safe offline location
2. Never share your private keys or recovery phrase with anyone
3. Create a strong and unique password for your MetaMask wallet
4. Consider using a hardware wallet in tandem with MetaMask for heightened security
5. Enable "phishing detection" in your MetaMask settings
6. Download wallet warning services like Fire to understand your transactions better

From Ottawa Web3 Starter Guide [Source S]:
1. Lock MetaMask wallet when not in use
2. Watch out for phishing attacks—scammers may pretend to be MetaMask
3. Use a hardware wallet (Ledger or Trezor) if holding significant crypto
4. Write down seed phrase on two different sheets of paper, keep in separate safe places
5. Consider using more durable material (wood or metal) instead of just paper for long-term storage
6. Bookmark metamask.io—never click links in emails claiming to be MetaMask support

## Summary for Maya

Setting up a cryptocurrency wallet like MetaMask is like opening a bank account in the digital world. The most important thing is writing down your 12-word seed phrase and keeping it safe—this is your master key that can recover your money if anything happens to your device. Never share this phrase with anyone, and be careful of scams that try to trick you into giving it up.

Start with MetaMask (a "hot wallet") for your first steps—it's free, easy to use, and perfect for learning. Later, if you hold significant crypto, consider getting a hardware wallet ("cold wallet") for extra security.
