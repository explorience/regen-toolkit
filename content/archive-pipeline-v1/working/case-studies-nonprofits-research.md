# Research Brief: Case Studies - Nonprofits in Web3

## Topic
Case studies showing how nonprofits are using web3 and crypto donations

## Audience
Grounded Regen (Maya) - permaculture teacher, community garden organizer, no crypto experience

## Key Concepts (Source-Cited)

### 1. Ukraine Crisis Response (2022)
- Ukrainian government raised $50M+ in crypto in first week of Russia invasion [Source: cryptoaltruism.org]
- NGOs (Save the Children, Project HOPE, International Rescue Committee) received crypto within hours
- Transfers recorded transparently onchain - every donation traceable
- Speed advantage: no banking delays or correspondent-bank risk reviews

### 2. Turkey/Syria Earthquake (2023)
- NGOs received thousands in ETH, USDT, USDC within first day
- Donor mobilization across continents at pace traditional pipelines rarely match

### 3. Stablecoins in Aid Delivery
- Mercy Corps Ventures: stablecoin rails cut delivery costs by 60% in Syria vs traditional money-transfer agents [Source: cryptoaltruism.org]
- Reliable in fragile environments with unstable banking

### 4. Growth Statistics
- $1B+ in crypto donated in 2024 (386% increase from previous year) [Source: The Giving Block]
- 70% of Forbes' Top 100 US charities now accept crypto (up from 12% in 2020) [Source: cryptoaltruism.org]
- Projections: $2.5B in crypto donations expected in 2025 [Source: The Giving Block]
- One in four people globally now hold crypto [Source: Nasdaq via cryptoaltruism.org]

### 5. Why Nonprofits Adopt Crypto
- Faster access to funds (minutes vs days)
- Lower fees and currency spread losses
- Transparent transactions (public ledger)
- Alignment with younger donor demographics
- Cross-border ease without currency complexity
