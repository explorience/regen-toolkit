# Gathering Patterns That Work

*Event formats and structures that build real community connection*

---

## Why Format Matters

The structure of your event determines who participates and how.

Some formats exclude. Some include. Some energize. Some drain.

This guide covers gathering patterns that build community.

---

## Core Patterns

### 1. Circles

Everyone in a circle. No head table. No stage.

**Best for:** 5-20 people, decision-making, sharing

**Why it works:**
- Everyone sees everyone
- Equal positioning
- Conversation, not presentation
- Builds trust

**How to facilitate:**
- Go around (or not)
- One person speaks at a time
- Optional: talking stick
- No interruptions

---

### 2. Fishbowl

4-5 people in inner circle, rest observing.

**Best for:** Demonstrations, interviews, demonstrations

**Why it works:**
- Models conversation
- Others can learn from observation
- Less pressure than full participation

**How to facilitate:**
- Inner circle discusses
- Outer circle watches silently
- Switch periodically

---

### 3. World Cafe

Small groups at tables, rotate.

**Best for:** Brainstorming, exploring topics

**Why it works:**
- Intimate conversations
- Diverse input
- Lots of ideas generated

**How to facilitate:**
- Questions at each table
- Groups of 4-5
- Rotate every 15-20 min
- Synthesize at end

---

### 4. Open Space

Self-organizing, multiple sessions.

**Best for:** Large groups, many topics

**Why it works:**
- People choose what to engage with
- Passion drives participation
- Emerges what matters

**How to facilitate:**
- Opening: set context
- Marketplace: propose sessions
- Sessions: happen in parallel
- Closing: share learnings

---

### 5. Breakout Groups

Large group splits into small.

**Best for:** Getting input, building connections

**Why it works:**
- More people talk
- Builds intimacy
- Easier to manage than large discussions

**How to facilitate:**
- Clear instructions
- Report back structure
- Time limits

---

## Choosing Your Pattern

| Size | Pattern | Purpose |
|------|---------|---------|
| 5-15 | Circle | Trust building |
| 10-30 | Breakout | Connection |
| 15-50 | World Cafe | Ideation |
| 20-100 | Fishbowl | Learning |
| 50+ | Open Space | Emergence |

---

## Essential Elements

### Opening

Set context:
- Why we're here
- What we hope to achieve
- How we'll work

### Welcoming

Make newcomers feel:
- Introduced
- Valued
- Not lost

### Conversation

Design for:
- Everyone participating
- Listening as much as speaking
- Respectful disagreement

### Closing

Include:
- Key takeaways
- Next steps
- Gratitude

---

## Common Mistakes

### 1. No Structure
"Let's just talk" = few talk, most observe

### 2. Too Much Structure
Rigid agenda = no organic connection

### 3. Ignoring Newcomers
Only veterans talk = no new blood

### 4. No Follow Up
Great event, nothing happens after = no momentum
