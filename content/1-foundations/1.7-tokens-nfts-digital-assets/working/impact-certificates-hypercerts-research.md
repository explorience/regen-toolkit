# Impact Certificates & Hypercerts Research

## Overview

Impact certificates and hypercerts are Web3 primitives designed to track, certify, and fund positive impact in the regenerative finance (ReFi) ecosystem. They provide a mechanism for retrospective funding of public goods and create new incentive structures for impact creation.

---

## What Are Hypercerts?

**Hypercerts** are digital tokens that represent the impact of projects' work. They are the Web3 standard for impact certification, developed by the Hypercerts Foundation.

### Core Components

A hypercert captures four key dimensions of work:

1. **Who** – The contributors involved
2. **What** – The scope of the work
3. **When** – The timeframe during which the work was completed
4. **Where** – The location of the work

### How Hypercerts Work

1. **Account for work**: Hypercerts make work clearly identifiable by recording contributors, scope, time, and location
2. **Add evidence and evaluations**: Reports, measurements, and evaluations can be submitted by projects themselves or independent third-party evaluators
3. **Sell impact**: Funders buy portions of the hypercerts to reward projects and claim the impact
4. **Recognize contributors**: Financial and non-financial contributors are publicly recognized on "hyperboards"

## Impact Certificates & Retroactive Funding

### The Problem with Traditional Public Goods Funding

Trillions of dollars are spent annually on public goods via governmental agencies, foundations, private donations, and corporate spending. However, effective tracking of this work is lacking—leaving funders uncertain about whether their investments create intended positive outcomes.

### The Solution: Retroactive Funding

**Retroactive funding** pays for public goods work *after* the impact has been realized. This approach:

- **Reduces uncertainty** for funders since they can evaluate completed work before deciding
- **Creates incentives** for creators to undertake high-impact, uncertain projects
- **Enables learning feedback loops** from successes and failures
- **Attracts talent** to the public goods sector through performance-based compensation

### Real-World Example

In February 2022, ProjectTogether in Berlin coordinated refugee relief for Ukraine. Funders paid for this work *after* it was completed—allowing for efficient resource allocation based on demonstrated impact rather than speculative promises.

## The Three Actor Groups

### 1. Creators of Public Goods
- Create hypercerts documenting their work
- Can sell hypercerts prospectively or retrospectively
- May receive funding before or after producing impact

### 2. Funders
- Purchase hypercerts to support and claim impact
- Buy retrospectively (after impact is demonstrated) or prospectively (before)
- Benefit from reduced uncertainty in retrospective purchases

### 3. Evaluators
- Independent third parties that assess impact claims
- Build reputation over time (similar to rating agencies)
- Provide differentiation and credibility to the funding system

## Implementation Details

### Technical Standard
- Hypercerts can be implemented as **NFTs**, specifically following the **ERC-1155 standard**
- Records are stored on public verifiable blockchains
- Data is immutable, enhancing transparency and traceability

### Splitting & Merging
A key feature is the ability to:
- **Split** hypercerts along different impact dimensions (e.g., CO2 reduction vs. community health improvements)
- **Merge** multiple hypercerts into aggregated impact claims

This allows different funders to support specific aspects of a project.

### AT Protocol
Hypercerts are being built on the **AT Protocol (ATProto)**, enabling decentralized and interoperable data storage.

## Connection to Regenerative Finance (ReFi)

Hypercerts are a foundational component of **Regenerative Finance (ReFi)**, which aims to:

- Create financially sustainable models for positive impact
- Enable continuous funding streams for public goods
- Build interconnected funding systems that reward value creation

They work alongside other ReFi mechanisms:
- **Quadratic Funding** (Gitcoin Grants)
- **Retroactive Public Goods Funding** (Protocol Labs, Gitcoin)
- **MRV (Measurement, Reporting, Verification)** tools

## Summary

Hypercerts represent a Web3-native solution to the challenge of funding and rewarding public goods. By enabling retroactive funding—paying for impact after it's created—they solve key coordination problems in traditional grant-based funding. The system creates powerful incentives for creators, funders, and evaluators to participate in a dynamic ecosystem that can potentially scale regenerative finance globally.
