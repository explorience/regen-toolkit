## Overview

This research covers the three main ways to obtain tokens and NFTs: creating them (minting), buying them, and earning them through various web3 activities. The content is structured for beginners with no prior blockchain experience.

## 1. Creating Tokens & NFTs (Minting)

### What is Minting?

**Minting** is the process of creating new tokens or NFTs on a blockchain. It's similar to a central bank printing money, but happens through smart contracts [Source P: Bankless Academy].

### How to Mint NFTs

According to Bankless Academy's "Understanding Ethereum Token Standards" lesson:

1. **Create an account on an NFT marketplace** like OpenSea or Blur
2. **Connect your crypto wallet** to the marketplace
3. **Upload your digital content** (art, music, etc.)
4. **Set metadata** (name, description, properties)
5. **Confirm the minting transaction** in your wallet

> "ERC-721 tokens are widely used to represent the ownership of unique digital and real-world assets. To get an ERC-721 token, create an account on an NFT marketplace like OpenSea or Blur and purchase any listed NFT." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Understanding Ethereum Token Standards]

### Creating Fungible Tokens (ERC-20)

Creating fungible tokens requires:
- Technical knowledge OR
- No-code tools (like token creators)

> "Can anyone launch a token? Yes. Ethereum is a permissionless ecosystem and anyone can launch a fungible or non-fungible token. However, you will need technical know-how or access to no-code tools." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Understanding Ethereum Token Standards]

### Minting rETH (Liquid Staking Token)

One accessible way to "create" tokens is by minting liquid staking tokens:

> "By locking Ether in one of their staking pools, you'll mint rETH. While minting rETH directly from the Rocket Pool smart contract has the largest impact on network decentralization, you can also buy it on a decentralized exchange." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Staking Ether]

## 2. Buying Tokens & NFTs

### Where to Buy Tokens

#### Centralized Exchanges (CEXs)
Platforms like Coinbase, Binance where you can buy crypto with fiat currency.

#### Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs)
Peer-to-peer platforms like Uniswap, Velodrome, SushiSwap where you swap crypto for tokens.

> "Decentralized Exchanges are a type of dApp that facilitate self-custody token swaps. Unlike Centralized Exchanges (CEXs), this type of swap also enables users to swap while retaining full ownership of their assets." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Swapping on a Decentralized Exchange]

#### Third-Party Onramps
Services like MoonPay, Ramp, Transak let you buy crypto directly and send to your wallet.

> "Third-party payment apps enable users to fund a wallet on Layer 2 from a bank account or a debit or credit card." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Funding a Wallet on Layer 2]

### How to Buy Tokens on a DEX

From Bankless Academy's walkthrough:

1. **Load the dApp** (e.g., Velodrome, Uniswap)
2. **Connect your wallet** using the 'Connect' button
3. **Approve the connection** in your wallet
4. **Switch to the right network** (e.g., Optimism, Ethereum)
5. **Select input and output tokens** (e.g., swap ETH for OP)
6. **Approve token permissions** - limit to trade size for security
7. **Confirm the transaction** in both the dApp and your wallet
8. **Check your balance** - import token addresses if needed

> "DEXs are a great start to your web3 journey because most dApps follow a user interface layout similar to DEXs, and use similar interactions with your self-custody wallet." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Swapping on a Decentralized Exchange]

### Where to Buy NFTs

> "To get an ERC-721 token, create an account on an NFT marketplace like OpenSea or Blur and purchase any listed NFT." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Understanding Ethereum Token Standards]

Popular NFT marketplaces:
- OpenSea
- Blur
- Foundation
- Rarible

### Buying on Layer 2 (Lower Fees)

> "Layer 2s offer users of all experience levels the opportunity to experiment with decentralized finance in a way that is often prohibitive on Mainnet. Because it costs mere pennies to transact on these networks, it's a great place to become familiar with the basic building blocks of decentralized finance." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Funding a Wallet on Layer 2]

## 3. Earning Tokens

### Staking

**Staking** involves locking tokens in a network to support its operations and earn rewards.

> "Staking on Ethereum is an action that allows anyone to help secure the network. You lock a portion of Ether on the network, aiding network validators, and earning rewards." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Staking Ether]

#### Types of Staking:

1. **Solo Staking**: Run your own validator node (requires 32 ETH)
2. **Staking Pools**: Join others to stake together (any amount)
3. **Liquid Staking**: Get a token representing your staked position

> "Using the rETH token, Explorers can get involved in staking with any amount of Ether." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Staking Ether]

#### Staking Rewards:

- Annual returns between **3% and 16%**
- Rewards depend on:
  - Network activity (more traffic = higher rewards)
  - Number of validator nodes (more nodes = rewards divided)

> "With rETH, staking rewards are added straight to the value of the token: If you hold 1 rETH today, you will still own 1 rETH in a year. It will just be worth more." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Staking Ether]

### DeFi Lending

You can earn interest by lending tokens through DeFi platforms:

> "You can also earn interest in DeFi, with trustless lending and borrowing platforms. These platforms connect lenders and borrowers, managing risk through onchain collateral and smart contracts. Stablecoin lenders can earn annual returns far higher than available in the traditional banking sector." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Understanding Stablecoins]

### Governance Token Rewards

Some protocols reward users with tokens for participation:

> "Governance token: Offers holders voting rights in governance decisions of a platform." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Understanding Ethereum Token Standards]

### NFT Earned Through Learning

Platforms like Bankless Academy reward learners with NFTs (badges) for completing lessons:

> "Bankless Academy's 'Datadisk Collectibles' work in the same way [as NFTs]." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Understanding Ethereum Token Standards]

## 4. Key Concepts for Beginners

### Token Standards (Reference)

| Standard | Type | Use Case |
|----------|------|----------|
| ERC-20 | Fungible | Tokens like USDC, UNI |
| ERC-721 | Non-fungible | NFTs, unique items |
| ERC-1155 | Multi-token | Games, mixed assets |

> "ERC-20 is a token standard for easily exchangeable (fungible) tokens. ERC-721 is a standard for unique (non-fungible) tokens, known as NFTs. ERC-1155 is a standard used for both fungible and non-fungible tokens in the same contract." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Understanding Ethereum Token Standards]

### Wallet Requirements

To buy, sell, or earn tokens, you need:
1. **A crypto wallet** (self-custody)
2. **Funds** (ETH for gas, tokens to trade)
3. **Connected to a network** (Mainnet or L2)

> "You'll need a crypto wallet to access the world of blockchains! A wallet is like a blockchain account, used to store cryptocurrency, log into blockchain apps, and more." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Creating a Crypto Wallet]

### Security: Token Allowances

When using dApps, you grant **token allowances** - permissions for smart contracts to access your tokens.

> "Token allowances refer to permissions granted to smart contracts to spend tokens from a wallet without further approval. They can be exploited by malicious actors if the user is not aware permissions are in place." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Managing Token Allowances]

**Best Practice:** Limit allowances to your trade amount.

> "We recommend limiting this to your trade size to limit future interactions with your tokens." [Source P: Bankless Academy - Swapping on a Decentralized Exchange]
