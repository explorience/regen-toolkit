## Key Concepts

### What is a Crypto Wallet?

A crypto wallet is a digital tool that lets you store and manage the keys needed to access your cryptocurrencies. It doesn't store crypto itself—your funds live on the blockchain. [Source S - Britannica]

A wallet is a way to store and transact with crypto, but it's also like a gateway to your identity and digital assets. It can act as your onchain resume, showcasing your interactions within the crypto space and qualifying you for new experiences like airdrops and NFT mints. [Source P - Bankless]

Unlike a regular wallet that holds cash, a crypto wallet doesn't actually hold any currency. It stores extremely long strings of letters and numbers—private keys—that can be paired with public keys on a blockchain to access your cryptocurrency or other digital assets. [Source S - Britannica]

### Private Keys and Public Keys

Crypto wallets store private keys—long strings of letters and numbers—that can be paired with public keys to enable access to cryptocurrency on a blockchain. [Source S - Britannica]

Your private key is like the password to your bank account. Never share it with anyone.

### Seed Phrases (Recovery Phrases)

A seed phrase is simply a backup of your wallet should it become unusable via theft or destruction. Perhaps your wallet got stuck in the washing machine. Or perhaps you were relying on a software wallet on a laptop that doesn't work any longer. [Source S - Ledger]

A seed phrase is a sequence of random words that stores the data required to access or recover cryptocurrency. Seed phrases are generated by crypto wallets and are crucial for the safety of digital assets. [Source S - Coinbase (via search)]

Think of the wallet as being like a password manager for crypto, and the seed phrase as being the master password. The reason recovery phrase is a series of 12 to 24 simple words (like "army," "energy," "fabric," "lucky," "opera," "stereo," "trash," or "void") rather than a numeric password is because long strings of numbers are difficult for humans to remember or even transcribe correctly. [Source S - Coinbase (via search)]

A recovery phrase is generally a list of 12 to 24 words randomly generated by your crypto wallet, and given in a specific order. [Source S - Brave]

This 12-24 word phrase must be kept secure—it's vital to never enter it into a computer or smartphone and to never share it with anyone as it allows anyone to restore your entire wallet. [Source S - Ledger]

If you lose access to a cryptocurrency hardware wallet, you can use the recovery phrase—a manufacturer-provided string of words, often 12 to 24 words in length—to access your cryptocurrency from another hardware or software device. [Source S - Britannica]

### Hot Wallets vs. Cold Wallets

**Hot wallets** are connected to the internet (software wallets, web-based wallets). They are more convenient but less secure. [Source S - Britannica]

**Cold wallets** are offline (hardware wallets, paper wallets). They are more secure but less convenient. [Source S - Britannica]

| Wallet Type | Convenience | Security | Price |
|-------------|-------------|----------|-------|
| Hardware wallet | Low | High | Moderate |
| Software wallet | Medium | Medium | Low or free |
| Web-based wallet | High | Low | Low or free |
| Paper wallet | Low | High | Free |

## Examples Maya Would Understand

### The Mailbox Analogy
Think of your crypto wallet like a mailbox. The public key is like your street address—anyone can know it and send you mail (crypto). The private key is like the key to your mailbox—only you should have it. If someone gets your key, they can take your mail. The seed phrase is like having a master copy of that key hidden somewhere safe. If you lose your keys, the master copy (seed phrase) lets you make a new one.

### The Garden Shed Analogy
Your crypto lives on the blockchain (like a shared community garden). Your wallet is like a lockbox that holds the key to your plot in that garden. The seed phrase is the backup copy of that key. If your lockbox breaks or gets stolen, the seed phrase lets you get a new lockbox and still access your garden plot.

### The Washing Machine Scenario (from Ledger)
Perhaps your wallet got stuck in the washing machine. Or perhaps you were relying on a software wallet on a laptop that doesn't work any longer. In these cases, a seed phrase gives you the option to restore the entire wallet on another device or interface. [Source S - Ledger]

## Gaps: What Sources DON'T Cover

### For Maya (a non-technical person), the following gaps exist:

1. **No step-by-step setup walkthrough** - Sources explain what wallets are conceptually but don't walk through actually creating one.

2. **No specific security advice for beginners** - They mention NOT sharing seed phrases but don't explain common scams (phishing emails, fake support, "urgent" messages).

3. **No explanation of gas fees** - Essential concept for actually using Ethereum/EVM wallets is not covered.

4. **No mention of "custodial" vs "non-custodial" in beginner-friendly terms** - The difference matters a lot for security.

5. **No discussion of what happens if you lose your seed phrase** - Only explains recovery, not what happens if you literally lose the paper.

6. **No resources for people who speak Spanish/other languages** - Bankless Academy may not be accessible to non-English speakers.

7. **No discussion of crypto's environmental impact** - Maya as a permaculture teacher might care about this; sources don't address it.

8. **No guidance on how much crypto to start with** - Don't address the psychological aspect of starting small.

9. **No explanation of what "the blockchain" actually is** - Assumes reader knows this already.

10. **No troubleshooting help** - What if a transaction gets stuck? What if you send to the wrong address?
