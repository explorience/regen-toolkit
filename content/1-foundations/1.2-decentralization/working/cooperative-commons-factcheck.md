## Verified Claims

### 1. Mondragon Worker Cooperative (Spain)
**Claim:** "The most famous example is Mondragon in the Basque Country of Spain, a federation of worker cooperatives spanning manufacturing, retail, finance, and education."

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED  
**Source:** Wikipedia, Mondragon Corporation official site  
**Details:** Mondragon Corporation is indeed a federation of worker cooperatives based in the Basque region of Spain, founded in 1956. It includes cooperatives across manufacturing, retail, finance, and education. The corporation employs over 73,000 people across 102 federated cooperatives.

---

### 2. Banco Palmas (Brazil)
**Claim:** "Community banks like Banco Palmas in Brazil offer micro-loans and have sometimes introduced community currencies. Founded in 1998 in a low-income neighborhood in Fortaleza, Banco Palmas created the Palma currency..."

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED  
**Source:** Wikipedia, Local Futures, Participedia  
**Details:** Banco Palmas was created in January 1998 by ASMOCONP in the Palmeira neighborhood in Fortaleza, Brazil. The Palma social currency was created in 2000 (some sources say 2002), indexed one-to-one with the Brazilian Real.

---

### 3. Greenpill Network Chapters
**Claim:** "The Greenpill Network offers a concrete example... Greenpill Chapters function as local cooperatives organized around regenerative principles."

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED  
**Source:** greenpill.network, Regen Coordination Hub, Gitcoin  
**Details:** Greenpill Network has approximately 24 local chapters worldwide. Chapters operate as local groups that bridge regenerative digital infrastructure with grassroots community efforts.

### 4. Elinor Ostrom's 8 Principles
**Claim:** "Elinor Ostrom's eight principles for governing commons work well in digital contexts..."

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED  
**Details:** The 8 principles are well-documented in Ostrom's work "Governing the Commons" (1990). The article correctly lists all 8 principles.

### 5. Community Supported Agriculture (CSA)
**Claim:** "Consumer cooperatives... Think of Community Supported Agriculture (CSA)—members pay farmers upfront for weekly shares..."

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED  
**Details:** CSA is a well-established model where members pay upfront for farm shares, sharing risks and rewards with farmers.

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED

## Minor Editorial Suggestions

1. **Precision:** The article mentions "farmers in Kenya and India collectively buy seeds and land" - while agricultural cooperatives do exist in these countries, specific examples could strengthen this claim.

2. **Dates:** Consider adding specific dates where known (e.g., "founded in 1956" for Mondragon).

3. **Technical Claims:** The statement "Smart contracts can automate enforcement of community rules" is conceptually accurate but smart contracts cannot actually "enforce" rules in the real world - they can only execute predefined code. This could be clarified.
