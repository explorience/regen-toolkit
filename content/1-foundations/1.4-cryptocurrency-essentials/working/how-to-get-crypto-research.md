## 1. Buying Cryptocurrency: Exchanges

### Centralized Exchanges (CEX)

A centralized exchange (CEX) is controlled by a singular group or entity, such as a privately held company or publicly traded corporation. The controlling entity is fully responsible for all aspects of the platform's business. [Source: Britannica Money]

**Key characteristics:**
- Users create accounts and the exchange holds funds in custody
- The exchange manages private keys on behalf of users
- More exposed to hacks, insider threats, or regulatory shutdowns since they hold user funds and private keys [Source: MoonPay]
- More user-friendly interfaces, especially for beginners
- Higher liquidity compared to DEXs
- Usually require identity verification (KYC)

**Popular centralized exchanges include:** Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, Gemini

### Decentralized Exchanges (DEX)

A decentralized exchange (DEX) is governed by a protocol rather than a central authority. Users trade directly from their own crypto wallet, giving them full control over their assets. [Source: BitPay]

**Key characteristics:**
- Users retain custody of their funds
- No need for identity verification (some don't require KYC)
- Lower risk of centralized control issues and restrictions
- Lower liquidity compared to centralized exchanges — transactions may take more time to execute [Source: CoinLedger]
- Sometimes more complex user interfaces
- Use automated market makers (AMMs) and liquidity pools
- Users must verify contract addresses manually before interacting

**Popular DEXs include:** Uniswap, PancakeSwap, Curve, Sushiswap

## 2. Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Trading

P2P crypto exchanges allow users to buy and sell cryptocurrency directly with each other, providing a decentralized trading experience. [Source: KuCoin Learn]

**How P2P works:**
- Users browse offers listed by other traders
- Each offer includes cryptocurrency exchange rates, trading fees, and accepted payment methods
- The platform typically holds funds in escrow during the transaction
- Once the buyer confirms payment, the seller releases the crypto

**Popular P2P platforms:**
- Paxful — known for accessibility and extensive payment methods
- LocalCoinSwap — offers escrow protection and high liquidity
- Cryptomus P2P
- BitValve

**Advantages of P2P:**
- Can offer more privacy (many require only email to start)
- Multiple payment methods available (bank transfers, PayPal, gift cards, etc.)
- Some P2P platforms offer no-KYC options
- Users can set their own rates

## 3. Ways to Earn Cryptocurrency

### Staking

Staking involves locking up cryptocurrency holdings to support a blockchain network's operations, particularly in proof-of-stake (PoS) systems. In return for staking their coins and helping to maintain the blockchain's security and consensus, participants receive staking rewards. [Source: CoinLedger]

**Key points:**
- Available on many PoS blockchains (Ethereum, Cardano, Solana, Polygon, etc.)
- Rewards vary by network — Ethereum offers around 3-8% APY typically
- Some exchanges offer staking services for convenience
- Requires a minimum amount of tokens in most cases
- Rewards are distributed automatically by the protocol

### Yield Farming

Yield farming allows users to deposit their assets into liquidity pools on DeFi platforms to earn rewards. [Source: CoinMetro]

**Key points:**
- Involves lending or staking cryptocurrency assets in liquidity pools
- Users receive liquidity provider (LP) tokens
- Yields can be higher than staking but come with greater risk
- Certain platforms allow LPs to stake their LP tokens, generating secondary yield [Source: Kraken]
- APY can reach 10% or higher, though rates fluctuate constantly

### Lending

Users can lend their cryptocurrency to borrowers through DeFi lending protocols and earn interest. As of late 2025, some platforms offered up to 10.3% APY for lending stablecoins like USDC. [Source: Investopedia]

### Airdrops

Airdrops are methods of distributing free tokens to users, typically as a marketing tool or reward for early adoption. [Source: Dicloak]

**Types of airdrops:**
- **Standard airdrops:** Free tokens sent to wallet addresses
- **Holder airdrops:** Distributed to users who hold a specific token
- **Bounty airdrops:** Rewards for completing tasks (social media engagement, referrals, etc.)
- **Snapshot airdrops:** Tokens awarded based on holdings at a specific time

Some airdrops have become more complex, requiring users to complete tasks such as executing transactions on testnets and interacting with DeFi protocols. [Source: Dicloak]

### Mining

Mining is the process of validating transactions and adding them to a blockchain ledger. It requires advanced computer equipment to solve highly complicated math puzzles. [Source: FTC]

**Key points:**
- Primarily associated with proof-of-work cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, etc.)
- Energy-intensive and requires specialized hardware
- Most individual mining is no longer profitable for major cryptocurrencies
- Mining pools allow individuals to combine resources

### Other Methods

- **Bounties:** Rewards for completing specific tasks for blockchain projects (bug hunting, translations, content creation)
- **Referral programs:** Many exchanges and platforms offer referral bonuses
- **Play-to-earn games:** Blockchain-based games that reward players with tokens
- **Work for crypto:** Some employers pay in cryptocurrency

## 4. Avoiding Cryptocurrency Scams

### Common Scam Types

**Investment scams:**
- So-called "investment managers" who contact you out of the blue and promise to grow your money
- Scammers pretending to be celebrities who claim to multiply any cryptocurrency you send them
- Guaranteed returns or big payout promises — nobody can make those guarantees
- Free money promises — always fake [Source: FTC]

**Business/government impersonators:**
- Scammers impersonating well-known companies (Amazon, Microsoft, FedEx, banks)
- Fake companies creating fraudulent crypto coins or tokens
- Scammers impersonating government agencies, law enforcement, or utility companies
- They claim there's a legal problem or fraud on your account and demand cryptocurrency [Source: FTC]

**Job scams:**
- Fake job listings on job sites related to crypto
- Jobs that require paying a fee in cryptocurrency
- Tasks involving converting cash to crypto for "clients" — the initial check is fake [Source: FTC]

**Romance scams:**
- Online "love interests" who want you to send money or cryptocurrency to help you invest
- If you meet someone on a dating site or app and they want to show you how to invest in crypto, that's a scam [Source: FTC]

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Only scammers demand payment in cryptocurrency — no legitimate business will demand you send crypto in advance
- Only scammers will guarantee profits or big returns
- Never mix online dating and investment advice
- Pressure tactics — if someone says an opportunity is ending soon, stop and investigate
- Unsolicited direct messages about investment opportunities
- Customer support reaching out to you first (scammers often do this)
- Websites full of misspellings, grammatical errors, and poor design
- Promises of guaranteed returns or risk-free profits [Source: FTC, Trust Wallet]

### Security Best Practices

**Password and authentication:**
- Create passwords with at least 12 characters mixing uppercase, lowercase, letters, numbers, and symbols
- Never reuse passwords across different platforms
- Enable biometric authentication (Face ID, fingerprint)
- Set devices to lock automatically after a few minutes of inactivity [Source: Trust Wallet]

**Seed phrase protection:**
- Write your seed phrase on paper — never take photos of it
- Don't store seed phrases in cloud services, email, or password managers
- Store physical backups in secure locations (safe, safety deposit box)
- Make multiple physical copies in different locations [Source: Trust Wallet]

**dApp and token safety:**
- Verify contract addresses manually — go directly to the project's official website
- Don't trust links from social media, Discord messages, or emails
- Check and revoke unused token approvals regularly using tools like Etherscan's token approval checker
- Set specific limits rather than unlimited approval when connecting to dApps [Source: Trust Wallet]

**Additional precautions:**
- Use watch-only accounts to monitor balances without exposing private keys
- Search online for company/person names plus words like "review," "scam," or "complaint" before investing
- Keep software and apps updated
- Test backups before storing large amounts of crypto [Source: Trust Wallet, FTC]

## Key Differences: Cryptocurrency vs. Traditional Currency

- Cryptocurrency accounts are not backed by a government — funds are not FDIC insured
- Cryptocurrency values change constantly and can be highly volatile
- Cryptocurrency payments do not come with legal protections like credit cards
- Cryptocurrency payments are typically not reversible
- Transactions are recorded on a public ledger (blockchain) [Source: FTC]
