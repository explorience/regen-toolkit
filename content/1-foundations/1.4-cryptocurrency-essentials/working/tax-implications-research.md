## Overview

Cryptocurrency is treated as **property** for U.S. tax purposes, not currency. This means every transaction involving digital assets can have tax implications similar to property or stock transactions. [Source P], [Source S]

---

## Key Tax Concepts

### Cost Basis
The cost basis is the total amount you spent acquiring cryptocurrency, including:
- Purchase price
- Transaction fees
- Brokerage commissions
- Other related costs

This is used to determine your taxable gains or losses. [Source P]

### Capital Gains vs. Losses
- **Short-term capital gain:** Asset held for one year or less
- **Long-term capital gain:** Asset held for more than one year

Short-term gains are taxed as ordinary income. Long-term gains have preferential tax rates. [Source S]

---

## Taxable vs. Non-Taxable Events

### Taxable Transactions
- **Selling cryptocurrency** for a profit or loss
- **Trading crypto for crypto** (e.g., BTC for ETH)
- **Using crypto to pay for goods/services**
- **Receiving crypto from mining or staking**
- **Receiving airdrops** as rewards or payments
- **Hard forks** that result in receiving new tokens [Source P], [Source S]

### Non-Taxable Transactions
- **Purchasing crypto with USD** (only taxed when sold)
- **Transferring crypto between your own wallets**
- **Donating crypto to charity**
- **Gifting crypto** (though gifts over $15,000 require a gift tax return) [Source P]

---

## NFT Taxation

### Buying NFTs
- **Using crypto to buy an NFT:** Treated as selling crypto for USD, then buying NFT — triggers capital gain/loss on the crypto sale
- **Using USD to buy an NFT:** Non-taxable event; USD paid becomes the cost basis [Source P]

### Reselling NFTs
- Selling an NFT for USD, trading it for other tokens, or using it to purchase something is a taxable event
- Some NFTs may be considered **collectibles** with a maximum 28% long-term capital gains rate (though there's strong argument NFTs aren't collectibles since they're not tangible property) [Source P]

### NFT Lending/Collateral
- Taking a loan or loaning NFTs is typically not a taxable event
- Interest earned is taxable income at fair market value
- Interest paid with cryptocurrency can trigger capital gain/loss [Source P]

---

## DeFi Taxation

### Borrowing
- Loans are not taxable income
- Paying back a loan is not deductible
- Paying interest with crypto is a taxable spend [Source P]

### Lending & Liquidity Pools
**With placeholder tokens (e.g., cETH):**
- Swapping crypto for pool tokens (e.g., ETH → cETH) is likely a taxable event
- Swapping back is also a taxable event
- Each triggers capital gain/loss [Source P]

**Without placeholder tokens (e.g., Celsius):**
- Depositing is not a taxable event
- Withdrawing is not a taxable event
- Interest earned is taxed as ordinary income [Source P]

### Governance Tokens
- Rewards (e.g., COMP tokens from Compound) are taxed as **ordinary income** [Source P]

### Wrapping Coins
- Wrapping (e.g., BTC → wBTC) is a legal gray area
- Conservative approach: treat as taxable token-to-token swap [Source P]

---

## Gas Fees

- **Making purchases:** Gas fees can be added to cost basis
- **Selling/disposing:** Gas fees can be subtracted from sales proceeds
- **Crypto-for-crypto trades:** Gas fees can be subtracted from the first token's proceeds OR added to second token's basis
- **Transferring between your own accounts:** Generally not part of capital gain calculations (conservative position) [Source P]

---

## DAO Taxation

- **DAO tokens increasing in value:** Not taxable until sold, traded, or disposed
- **Receiving income/dividends in crypto:** Taxed as ordinary income
- **Form K-1:** Some DAOs structured as partnerships issue K-1 forms to members
- **Airdrops:** Taxed like other airdrops — typically as ordinary income [Source P]

## IRS Reporting Requirements

### The Digital Assets Question
You must answer "Yes" or "No" on your tax return if you:
- Received digital assets (as reward, award, or payment)
- Sold, exchanged, or disposed of a digital asset [Source S]

### Forms Used
- **Form 8949:** Report crypto trades and sales
- **Schedule D:** Report overall capital gains/losses
- **Schedule 1:** Report crypto-related income (mining, staking, airdrops)
- **Form 709:** For gifting digital assets over $15,000
- **Form 1099-DA:** Broker reporting (required for transactions after Jan 1, 2025) [Source P], [Source S]

### Record Keeping
Maintain records of:
- Purchase, receipt, sale, exchange, or disposition of all digital assets
- Fair market value in USD at time of each transaction [Source S]

---

## Important IRS Updates (2024-2025)

### Broker Reporting (Form 1099-DA)
- **Jan 1, 2025+:** Brokers must report gross proceeds
- **Jan 1, 2026+:** Brokers must report basis on certain transactions
- Applies to custodial exchanges, hosted wallets, kiosks, payment processors [Source S]

### Transactions with Temporary Reporting Exceptions
The IRS has delayed reporting requirements for:
- Wrapping/unwrapping transactions
- Liquidity provider transactions
- Staking transactions
- Digital asset lending
- Short sales
- Notional principal contracts [Source S]

## Summary Table

| Transaction | Tax Treatment |
|-------------|---------------|
| Buy crypto with USD | Not taxable |
| Sell crypto for USD | Capital gain/loss |
| Trade crypto for crypto | Capital gain/loss |
| Use crypto to buy goods/services | Capital gain/loss |
| Receive from mining/staking | Ordinary income |
| Receive airdrop | Ordinary income |
| Earn interest (DeFi) | Ordinary income |
| Transfer between your wallets | Not taxable |
| Donate to charity | Not taxable (deductible) |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Crypto = Property** for tax purposes, not currency
2. **Every trade/transaction** should be recorded for tax purposes
3. **Cost basis tracking** is essential for accurate tax reporting
4. **DeFi and NFTs** create complex tax situations — consider professional advice
5. **Keep detailed records** of all transactions, dates, and values
6. **Non-custodial activities** (DeFi, self-custody) currently have less IRS guidance but should still be reported [Source P], [Source S]
