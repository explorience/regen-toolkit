## Summary

This document covers the history of Bitcoin — the first decentralized cryptocurrency. It presents factual information about Bitcoin's origins, key milestones, and the technological precursors that made it possible.

---

## Predecessors to Bitcoin

Before Bitcoin existed, several digital cash technologies laid the groundwork:

- **David Chaum's ecash (1983):** Introduced issuer-based digital cash protocols with privacy-preserving features
- **Adam Back's hashcash (1997):** Developed a proof-of-work scheme originally designed for spam control
- **Wei Dai's b-money (1998):** Proposed the first distributed digital scarcity-based cryptocurrency concept
- **Nick Szabo's bit gold (1998/2005):** Created a framework for collectible market-based digital tokens with proof-of-work
- **Hal Finney's RPOW (2004):** Developed reusable proof-of-work using hashcash as the proof algorithm

These innovations solved pieces of the puzzle — but none created a fully functioning decentralized货币.

## The Birth of Bitcoin

### 2008: The Whitepaper

- **August 2008:** The domain bitcoin.org was registered
- **October 31, 2008:** A person using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto published a whitepaper titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" to a cryptography mailing list

The whitepaper proposed a peer-to-peer network using proof-of-work to record a public history of transactions — solving the double-spend problem without requiring a central bank.

### 2009: Launch

- **January 3, 2009:** Satoshi Nakamoto mined the genesis block (block number 0), creating the first 50 bitcoins
- **Embedded in the genesis block:** The text "The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks" — a reference to a headline from The Times newspaper, serving as both a timestamp and commentary on fractional-reserve banking
- **January 9, 2009:** The first open-source bitcoin client was released on SourceForge
- **January 12, 2009:** The first bitcoin transaction occurred — Satoshi sent 10 BTC to programmer Hal Finney (block 170)

### Early Supporters

Early adopters included:
- **Hal Finney** — received the first transaction, downloaded the software on launch day
- **Wei Dai** — creator of b-money, a Bitcoin predecessor
- **Nick Szabo** — creator of bit gold, another predecessor

Satoshi is estimated to have mined approximately 1 million bitcoins in the early period.

## Key Milestones

### 2010: First Retail Transaction

- **May 22, 2010:** The first notable retail transaction involving physical goods occurred. Laszlo Hanyecz paid 10,000 BTC (worth ~$40 at the time) for two pizzas delivered from Papa John's in Jacksonville, Florida. This date is celebrated as "Bitcoin Pizza Day."

### 2010: Security Incident

- **August 6, 2010:** A major vulnerability was discovered in the Bitcoin protocol — a transaction could have outputs exceeding its inputs due to integer overflow
- **August 15, 2010:** The vulnerability was exploited in a single transaction that created 92 billion bitcoins
- **Within hours:** Developers fixed the bug and miners forked the blockchain to reverse the exploit

### 2011: Mainstream Adoption Begins

- **January 2011:** The Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) began accepting bitcoin donations (later paused in June 2011, then resumed in May 2013)
- **May 2011:** BitPay was founded to provide bitcoin payment processing for businesses
- **June 2011:** WikiLeaks and other organizations began accepting bitcoin donations

### 2012: Cultural Recognition

- **January 2012:** Bitcoin was featured in a fictionalized trial on the CBS legal drama "The Good Wife" (episode "Bitcoin for Dummies")

---

## Satoshi Nakamoto's Disappearance

- **Mid-2010:** Satoshi Nakamoto progressively reduced involvement in Bitcoin
- **April 2011:** Satoshi communicated with a Bitcoin contributor, stating they had "moved on to other things"
- Satoshi handed over development leadership to **Gavin Andresen**, who became the lead developer and the Bitcoin community's most recognizable public figure

The real identity of Satoshi Nakamoto remains unknown. Speculated candidates have included:
- Wei Dai
- Nick Szabo  
- Hal Finney (who denied it)
- Various others investigated by journalists — none confirmed
