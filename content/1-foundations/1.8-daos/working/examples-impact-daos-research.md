# Examples of Impact DAOs - Research

## Overview

This document compiles factual information about impact DAOs from three source organizations: SuperBenefit Knowledge Garden, ReFi DAO, and Greenpill. The content covers examples of impact DAOs, their activities, and why they matter.

---

## Source: SuperBenefit Knowledge Garden

### About SuperBenefit

SuperBenefit is a DAO that researches and incubates initiatives harnessing web3 technologies (DAOs, NFTs, DeFi) in pursuit of systems transformation. Their core purpose is building a repository of DAOs and web3 projects that go beyond charity/philanthropy impact and align with agreed-upon definitions of "systems transformation."

### Key Resources

- **Knowledge Garden**: A collaborative digital space serving as both an internal tool for DAO learning and a contribution to the wider knowledge commons. Organized into four areas:
  - **Artifacts**: Polished and validated knowledge including finished research reports, project documentation, and community-validated knowledge
  - **Lexicon**: Shared vocabulary with term definitions and common concepts
  - **Library**: Curated external resources including referenced articles, papers, and relevant external projects
  - **Notes**: Works in progress including project-specific documentation and research in progress

- **DAO Systems Transformation Repository/Library**: A project focused on building a repository of DAOs and web3 projects that align with systems transformation definitions. Uses the DAO Cell model to guide its work.

### Systems Transformation Framework

SuperBenefit contributors have been developing a draft systems transformation framework through weekly meetups. The framework analyzes what has enabled and constrained DAOs' systems transformation aspirations.

## Source: ReFi DAO

### About ReFi DAO

ReFi DAO is building a global regenerative economy rooted in local communities. They coordinate a network of place-based nodes, initiatives, and contributors using Web3 to restore ecosystems.

### Core Activities

ReFi DAO Global provides the following support to the network:

- **Sense-making**: Understanding the regenerative finance landscape and mapping opportunities for impact
- **Education**: Spreading knowledge about ReFi through workshops, resources, and community learning
- **Opportunity Development**: Connecting regenerative projects with resources, partners, and funding opportunities
- **Fundraising Support**: Helping regenerative projects access funding through grants, donations, and partnerships
- **Onboarding & Empowerment**: Supporting new participants in joining the regenerative finance movement
- **Network Building**: Weaving connections across local nodes and global movements for collective impact

### ReFi Local Nodes

ReFi DAO's network consists of decentralized, community-driven Local Nodes that address local challenges while contributing to a shared vision. As of 2024, the network includes nodes such as:

- **ReFi Costa Rica**: Merging crypto philanthropy with grassroots activism
- **ReFi Barcelona**: Innovative cooperative structure
- **ReFi BayArea**: Integration of real estate and solar energy into regenerative systems
- **ReFi Lagos**: Leading reforestation efforts
- **ReFi Sicilia**: Environmental restoration initiatives
- **ReFi Tanzania**: Addressing urban waste challenges through verification and cleanup systems
- **ReFi Mexico**: Developing tools like the Impact Market Maker

### Node Activities

ReFi Local Nodes engage in diverse activities including:
- Crypto Cantinas (community gatherings combining crypto education with local activism)
- Web3 workshops (e.g., workshops in Uganda)
- Reforestation campaigns
- Urban regeneration projects
- Digital innovation initiatives

### ReFi Local Node Toolkit

A comprehensive resource for supporting Local Nodes and the broader regenerative community. The toolkit provides guidance for starting and operating place-based regenerative finance initiatives.

### 2024 Achievements

- **ReFi Local Node Incubator**: Empowered over 100 participants through a 13-session program
- **Regen Coordination Genesis**: Distributed over $236,000 to grassroots communities including Local Nodes
- **Celo Public Goods Partnership**: Supported climate-focused funding initiatives and programs like BioFi Pathfinders Round

### 2025 Initiatives

- **Impact Data Commons**: A scalable system for tracking and reporting impact across the ecosystem
- **Regen Coordination Local ReFi Toolkit**: Expanding the toolkit to serve Local Node and broader regenerative community needs
- **Prosperity Passport**: An on-chain identity and reputation platform to recognize contributions

## Source: Greenpill

### About Greenpill

Greenpill is a movement focused on "Turning Degens to Regens" — building a CoordiNation across nations and cultures. The initiative exports regenerative digital infrastructure to the world through local chapters.

### Structure

Greenpill operates through local chapters worldwide. As of 2023, approximately 24 local chapters exist across different regions. Chapters organize activities at the grassroots level to bring regenerative web3 to local communities.

### Resources

#### Books/Guides

- **Pathways to Regeneration**: A bridge between historical wisdom and transformative potential of modern economic technologies
- **Ethereum Localism**: Celebrates the web3 movement exploring how to fund what matters locally
- **Grassroots Economics**: Explores capital allocation in Kenya and how it could translate to DAOs
- **Onchain Capital Allocation**: A practical journey from present mechanisms to future possibilities
- **Impact DAOs (2022)**: Focuses on web3 projects with positive impact on the world
- **Onchain Impact Networks (2024)**: Continues the exploration of impact-focused web3 networks
- **Greenpill v0**: How crypto could regenerate the world
- **Future History of the Open Internet**: Celebrates history of Open Source Software and peer-to-peer coordination
- **Ethereum for Public Goods Series**: Using web3 to fight Moloch (the God of Coordination Failure)
- **Exploring MycoFi**: Mycelial design patterns for web3 and beyond
- **Stuff Crypto OGs Know**: Teaching people how to survive, thrive, and regenerate in the crypto ecosystem
- **Local Regen Guide**: Teaching engaging ways to regenerate local communities

#### Podcast

The Greenpill Podcast dives deeper into the regenerative crypto-economic frontier, releasing new episodes Tuesday and Thursday.

### Participation Levels

- **Beginner**: Education and social engagement
- **Intermediate**: Tell a friend
- **Advanced**: Start an ImpactDAO, distribute books/podcasts/memes, organize teachbacks, run regenerative experiments in local communities

### Greenpill Dev Guild

A community of builders supporting Greenpill Chapters and regen communities with regenerative tools, guides, and workshops focused on impact measurement, capital allocation, and community coordination.

## Additional Impact DAO Examples

From research across these sources, several specific impact DAOs are commonly referenced:

### Klima DAO

- Focuses on tokenizing carbon offsets
- Uses the Toucan Carbon Bridge to bring carbon offsets on-chain in tokenized form
- Functions as a decentralized market-maker for tokenized carbon pools
- Enables price discovery for different classes of carbon assets

### Toucan

- Provides the carbon bridge protocol that connects real-world carbon credits to blockchain
- Allows users to bridge carbon credits after verification through Verra
- Creates TC02 tokens (ERC20 compatible) that can be used in DeFi protocols

### Regen Network

- A domain-specific proof-of-stake blockchain built on Cosmos SDK
- Designed to catalyze ecological regeneration through incentivized soil health and carbon sequestration
- Empowers communities to coordinate, fund, and verify regenerative action at scale
- Issues ecological assets through peer-reviewed protocols with blockchain-based verification

### Gitcoin

- Implements quadratic funding for public goods
- Funds projects that contribute to open source and public goods
- Vision includes Impact DAOs augmenting every income on the planet to help communities thrive

## Why Impact DAOs Matter

Based on the sources, impact DAOs matter for several reasons:

1. **Systems Transformation**: SuperBenefit emphasizes that impact DAOs can go beyond charity/philanthropy to achieve systemic change

2. **Local-to-Global Networks**: ReFi DAO demonstrates how decentralized networks can enable place-based action while contributing to global regeneration goals

3. **Community Empowerment**: These organizations equip communities with tools and knowledge to participate in regenerative economies

4. **Technological Innovation**: Impact DAOs implement cutting-edge tools like Web3 for transparent tracking and scaling of regenerative activities

5. **New Economic Models**: Greenpill explores how crypto-economic systems can create positive externalities for neighbors and the world
