# Ethereum Ecosystem Research

## Overview

The Ethereum ecosystem is the largest and most actively developed blockchain ecosystem in the crypto space. It serves as the foundation for decentralized finance (DeFi), NFTs, DAOs, and thousands of decentralized applications (dApps). [Source P: Bankless Academy]

## 1. Ethereum Blockchain (Layer 1)

### What is Ethereum?
Ethereum is a decentralized, open-source blockchain with smart contract functionality. It was proposed by Vitalik Buterin in 2013 and launched in 2015. Unlike Bitcoin, which is primarily a store of value, Ethereum functions as a programmable platform for building decentralized applications. [Source P: Bankless Academy]

### ETH - The Native Token
ETH (Ether) is the native cryptocurrency of Ethereum. It serves two critical roles:
- **Gas**: ETH pays for transaction fees and computational services on the network
- **Staking**: ETH secures the network through proof-of-stake consensus

ETH is often called "digital oil" because its value derives from fundamental utility—it's required for all transactions, smart contracts, dApps, and rollup operations across the Ethereum ecosystem. [Source P: Bankless]

### The Merge & Current Upgrades
Ethereum completed "The Merge" in September 2022, transitioning from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake. This reduced energy consumption by approximately 99.95%. Subsequent upgrades include:
- **Shanghai**: Enabled ETH withdrawals from the staking contract
- **Dencun**: Introduced "blobs" for cheaper L2 data storage (EIP-4844)
- **Fusaka**: Further scaling improvements with enhanced blob capacity and L1 optimizations

## 2. Layer 2 Scaling Solutions

### Why Layer 2?
Ethereum's Layer 1 can process approximately 15-30 transactions per second (TPS). To achieve mass adoption, Layer 2 (L2) solutions handle transactions off-chain while inheriting Ethereum's security. [Source P: Bankless Academy]

### Types of L2s

#### Optimistic Rollups
Assume transactions are valid by default but allow fraud proofs:
- **Optimism**: One of the first major L2s, focused on simplicity and speed
- **Arbitrum**: Largest L2 by TVL, known for strong developer tooling
- **Base**: Coinbase's L2, launched in 2023, rapidly growing ecosystem

#### ZK Rollups
Use zero-knowledge proofs for validity:
- **zkSync Era**: Matter Labs' zkEVM solution
- **Starknet**: StarkWare's validity rollup, using STARKs
- **Polygon zkEVM**: Polygon Labs' zkEVM implementation

### Major L2 Networks by TVL
As of 2026, the largest L2s include:
1. Arbitrum
2. Optimism
3. Base
4. zkSync Era
5. Starknet

L2Beat tracks over 140 live Layer-2 networks. Combined, L2s now process more transaction volume than Ethereum mainnet. [Source P: CoinDesk, L2Beat]

## 3. Decentralized Finance (DeFi)

### What is DeFi?
DeFi refers to financial services built on public blockchains, eliminating intermediaries like banks. Key DeFi primitives include:

### Major DeFi Protocols on Ethereum

#### Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs)
- **Uniswap**: Pioneering automated market maker (AMM), allowing token swaps directly from wallets
- **1inch**: DEX aggregator finding best rates across multiple DEXs

#### Lending & Borrowing
- **Aave**: Non-custodial liquidity protocol enabling lending and borrowing
- **Compound**: Algorithmic interest rate protocol

#### Stablecoins
- **USDC**: Center-consortium backed stablecoin
- **DAI**: Decentralized, crypto-collateralized stablecoin

### DeFi Stats
The Ethereum ecosystem hosts 364+ DeFi applications with billions in total value locked (TVL). [Source S: ethereum-ecosystem.com]

## 5. DAOs & Governance

### What are DAOs?
Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs) are blockchain-based organizations governed by smart contracts and token holder voting.

### DAO Infrastructure
- **MakerDAO**: Governs DAI stablecoin
- **Compound DAO**: Governs Compound protocol
- **Optimism Collective**: Retroactive public goods funding

The ecosystem includes 66+ DAO and governance tools. [Source S: ethereum-ecosystem.com]

## 7. The Rollup-Centric Roadmap

Ethereum's scaling strategy centers on rollups:
- **L2s as authorization networks**: Handle transaction execution
- **L1 as settlement layer**: Final settlement and data availability
- **PeerDAS**: Upcoming upgrade to increase blob capacity ~8x

This architecture aims to support millions of TPS while maintaining decentralization. [Source P: Bankless]
