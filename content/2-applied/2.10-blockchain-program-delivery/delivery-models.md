# Delivery Models for Blockchain Programs

*How to structure and deliver blockchain education and adoption programs in your community.*

---

## Overview

Blockchain program delivery isn't one-size-fits-all. The best delivery model depends on your audience, resources, and goals. This guide covers the main approaches and when to use each.

## Model 1: Workshop Series

A structured sequence of hands-on sessions, typically 4-8 weeks, covering progressive topics.

**Format**: Weekly 2-hour sessions. Each builds on the previous.
**Best for**: Committed learners. People who want depth.
**Requirements**: Consistent facilitator, venue, materials.
**Typical curriculum**: Week 1: What is blockchain? Week 2: Wallets & keys. Week 3: DeFi basics. Week 4: Community governance. Week 5: Building your first project.

## Model 2: Drop-In Clinics

Open sessions where anyone can show up with questions or projects. No curriculum — responsive to what people need.

**Format**: Weekly or biweekly 1-2 hour sessions. Open door.
**Best for**: Communities with mixed experience levels. Ongoing support.
**Requirements**: Knowledgeable facilitator, flexible venue.
**Strengths**: Low barrier, meets people where they are.

## Model 3: Peer Learning Circles

Small groups (4-8 people) who learn together through shared materials and discussion. No teacher — everyone contributes.

**Format**: Weekly meetings, shared readings/videos, structured discussion.
**Best for**: Self-directed learners. Building shared vocabulary.
**Requirements**: Curated materials, rotating facilitation.

## Model 4: Mentorship Program

Experienced members paired with newcomers for 1-on-1 guidance over 1-3 months.

**Format**: Weekly check-ins, practical assignments, ongoing support.
**Best for**: Deep skill transfer. Building relationships.
**Requirements**: Willing mentors with time, clear expectations.

## Model 5: Bootcamp/Intensive

Concentrated learning over a short period (weekend, week-long).

**Format**: Full-day or multi-day sessions. Immersive.
**Best for**: Getting a cohort up to speed quickly.
**Requirements**: Significant facilitator time, venue, materials.

## Choosing a Model

| Factor | Workshop | Drop-In | Peer Circle | Mentorship | Bootcamp |
|--------|----------|---------|-------------|------------|----------|
| Commitment | High | Low | Medium | Medium | High (short) |
| Depth | High | Medium | Medium | High | High |
| Scale | Medium | High | Low | Low | Medium |
| Cost | Medium | Low | Low | Low | High |
| Best outcome | Skills | Support | Understanding | Relationship | Momentum |

## Hybrid Approaches

Most successful programs combine models:
- Monthly workshop + weekly drop-in
- Bootcamp to launch, then peer circles to sustain
- Mentorship alongside group learning

## Moving Forward

Start with what you can sustain. A consistent monthly drop-in is better than an ambitious workshop series that dies after 3 sessions.
