# Operational Security Basics

**Your safety is in your hands.** In web3, you are your own bank. That means you control your money, but you also responsibility to keep it safe. The good news? A few simple habits protect you from most threats. Let's learn them together.

---

## Why Security Matters Here

When you use crypto and web3 tools, there's no bank to call if something goes wrong. No customer support number to dial. If someone gets access to your wallet, they can take your funds — and there's often no way to get them back.

But here's the powerful part: **you** are the strongest protection your funds have. Most attacks succeed not because systems are broken, but because people don't know the simple habits that keep them safe.

---

## Your First Line of Defense: Your Secret Phrase

When you create a crypto wallet, you'll get a secret phrase — usually 12 or 24 words. This phrase is **the key to everything**. Anyone who has it can access your wallet and take your funds.

**What to do:**
- **Write it down** on paper. Not in your phone, not in a file on your computer.
- **Keep it somewhere safe** — a locked drawer, a hidden place only you know.
- **Never share it** with anyone. Not your best friend. Not someone who says they're "support." Not anyone.

**What never to do:**
- Never type your secret phrase into any website or app.
- Never take a photo of it.
- Never send it to yourself via email or messaging apps.
- Never enter it on a website someone messages you.

---

## Passwords: Your Daily Shield

Strong, unique passwords protect your accounts. This sounds basic, but it's your daily shield.

**Good password habits:**
- Use long passwords — 12 characters or more. A phrase like "MyCatLovesRunning2024!" is stronger than "cat123."
- Use different passwords for different accounts. If one gets breached, the others stay safe.
- Use a password manager if you can. It remembers passwords so you don't have to.

**On your phone:**
- Turn on screen lock (PIN, fingerprint, or face recognition).
- Don't use simple PINs like 1234 or your birthday.
- Keep your phone's software updated — updates often fix security problems.

## Spotting Scams: Your Most Important Skill

Most attacks try to trick you, not hack your phone. Learning to spot scams protects you more than any password.

### Common Scam Types

**Fake support:** Someone messages you pretending to be support from a wallet or exchange. Real support never asks for your secret phrase or password.

**Phishing links:** A message says "Click here to claim your tokens" or "Verify your wallet." The link looks real but steals your info. Always check the website URL carefully.

**Too-good-to-be-true offers:** "Send me 0.1 ETH and I'll send you 1 ETH back!" — this is always a scam. Nobody gives free money.

**Fake apps:** Download wallet apps only from official app stores. Check the developer name and reviews.

### How to Protect Yourself

- **Verify everything.** Double-check URLs. Verify that messages are really from who they say they are.
- **Don't rush.** Scammers create urgency: "Act now or lose your funds!" Real opportunities don't pressure you.
- **When in doubt, ask.** Talk to someone you trust before clicking links or sending money.

## Protecting Your Funds

### Keep Things Separate

Consider using different wallets for different purposes:
- A **savings wallet** with most of your funds — keep this secret phrase extra safe
- A **spending wallet** with small amounts for daily use
- This way, if one is compromised, you don't lose everything.

### Be Careful with Networks

When sending crypto, always check:
- The **network** (Ethereum, Base, etc.). If someone says "send to this address" on the wrong network, you could lose your funds forever.
- The **address** — copy and paste it, then double-check the first and last few characters.

### Update Regularly

Keep your wallet apps and phone software updated. Updates often patch security holes that attackers exploit.

---

## Social Media Safety

Scammers often target people through social media or messaging apps.

**What to do:**
- Be private. Set your accounts to private so strangers can't see your activity.
- Don't announce how much crypto you have.
- Be skeptical of direct messages from people you don't know.

**What to avoid:**
- Don't click links DMed to you, even from friends (their account might be compromised).
- Don't join giveaways that ask for your wallet address or secret phrase.

---

## What If Something Goes Wrong?

If you think your wallet might be compromised:

1. **Act fast.** Transfer your remaining funds to a new wallet immediately.
2. **Don't panic.** The sooner you act, the more you can save.
3. **Learn from it.** Every security experience makes you stronger.

You don't need to be a tech expert. You just need to be careful and aware. Share what you've learned with friends in your community. Together, you're stronger.
