# Scam Prevention for Communities

*Guide to scam prevention for communities*

---

## How to protect your community from common Web3 scams.

## Common Scams Targeting Communities

### Fake Token Launches
Someone creates a token claiming affiliation with your community. Members buy it thinking it's official. It's not.

**Prevention**: Clearly communicate official token addresses. Use verified channels only.

### Governance Attacks
Malicious actors acquire voting power and pass proposals that drain treasury.

**Prevention**: Timelocks on large transactions. Quorum requirements. Multi-sig approval.

### Phishing Targeting Community Channels
Fake announcements in Discord/Telegram asking members to "verify" wallets.

**Prevention**: Admin-only announcements. Clear communication about what you will and won't ask for. Multiple admin accounts.

### Impersonation
Someone pretends to be a community leader and requests funds or access.

**Prevention**: Verify identity through multiple channels. Never share credentials via DM.

## Building a Security Culture

### Education
- Regular security reminders
- Share examples of scams (anonymized)
- New member orientation includes security basics

### Process
- No single person controls significant funds
- Multi-sig for treasury
- Timelocks on large transactions
- Clear approval workflows

### Response Plan
When a scam or breach occurs:
1. Secure remaining funds immediately
2. Alert all members through verified channels
3. Document what happened
4. Report to relevant authorities
5. Learn and update procedures

## Moving Forward
Security is everyone's responsibility. Build awareness into your community culture, not just your technology.
