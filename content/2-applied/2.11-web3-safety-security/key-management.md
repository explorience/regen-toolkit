# Key Management for Communities

*Guide to key management for communities*

---

## How to manage cryptographic keys safely as a community.

## What Are Keys?
Private keys are what give you control over your crypto assets. Whoever has the key, controls the funds. In community contexts, this creates unique challenges: how do you share control without sharing keys?

## Individual Key Management

### Best Practices
1. **Never share private keys or seed phrases**
2. **Use hardware wallets** for significant amounts
3. **Write seed phrases on paper** (not digital)
4. **Store backups in separate locations**
5. **Test recovery** before trusting large amounts

### Common Mistakes
- Storing seed phrases in notes apps or photos
- Using the same password for everything
- Not testing backup recovery
- Sharing keys with "trusted" people

## Community Key Management

### Multi-Signature Wallets
The primary solution for community funds. Multiple people hold keys; a threshold must agree to transact.

### Key Ceremonies
For creating important community wallets:
1. Meet in person
2. Each signer generates their key independently
3. Verify the multisig configuration together
4. Test with small amounts
5. Document the process (not the keys!)

### Succession Planning
What happens if a key holder:
- Loses their key?
- Becomes unavailable?
- Leaves the community?

Plan for these scenarios before they happen.

## Moving Forward
Take key management seriously. It's not paranoia — it's responsibility. The community's funds depend on it.
