# Funding Your Local Node

*How to finance your community finance system without losing your community*

---

## Why Funding Matters

Money is uncomfortable to talk about. But local nodes need resources — even small ones.

The question isn't whether to fund your node. It's how to fund it in a way that matches your values.

The Greenpill Local Regen Guide emphasizes community-owned and community-governed finance [1]. Your funding model should support that vision, not undermine it.

---

## The Funding Spectrum

Local nodes can be funded in many ways. The right approach depends on your scale, goals, and community preferences.

Let's walk through the options, from simplest to most complex.

---

## Free: The Grassroots Approach

### Member Contributions
The simplest: each member contributes time or money.

Examples:
- $5-20/month from each member
- Skills contributed instead of money
- Equipment or space donations

Pros:
- Simple to start
- Builds ownership
- Keeps everyone equal

Cons:
- Limits who can participate
- Can feel like "another subscription"

How to set amounts:
- Ask what feels comfortable
- Start low, increase later
- Have a "give what you can" option

### Sweat Equity
Members contribute labor instead of money.

Examples:
- Everyone helps with events
- Skills shared freely
- Manual record-keeping

This approach works well for communities with more time than money.

Pros:
- Everyone can participate regardless of income
- Builds stronger connections

Cons:
- Can burn people out
- Hard to measure value

---

## Low-Cost: Minimal Structures

### Use Free Tools
You don't need expensive software to run a local node:

- **Communication:** Discord, Signal, WhatsApp
- **Tracking:** Google Sheets, Notion
- **Meetings:** Library rooms, cafe back sections, someone's living room
- **Storage:** Members' homes

Cost: $0-50/month

### Barter and Exchange
Trade without money entirely:

- Skills for skills
- Items for items
- Time for time

This is how timebanks work. It's also how most communities function informally.

### Community Kitchen
If your node involves food:
- Potluck model
- Shared shopping (each brings something)
- Sliding scale contributions

---

## Medium: Building Capacity

### Membership Fees
Formalize contributions with monthly or annual fees.

This is how cooperatives typically work. Members pay to participate.

How to set fees:
1. Calculate your actual costs
2. Divide by expected members
3. Add a buffer for growth
4. Discuss with community
5. Start low, adjust later

Example calculation:
- Meeting space: $50/month
- Tools/supplies: $20/month
- Buffer: $30/month
- 10 members = $10/month each

### Tiered Membership
Different levels for different contributions:

| Tier | Fee | Includes |
|------|-----|----------|
| Basic | $5/mo | Voting, basic access |
| Supporter | $15/mo | Basic + events |
| Patron | $50/mo | All + priority |

This lets people give more if they want without forcing anyone.

### Service Fees
If your node provides services, you might charge:

- Small transaction fees (1-3%)
- Event admission
- Resource rental
- Workshop fees

Keep fees low. The goal isn't profit — it's sustainability.

### Local Revenue Generation
Make money locally:

- Sell goods (artisan products, food)
- Run events (film nights, workshops)
- Rent equipment
- Offer services (accounting, design)

This keeps money in the community.

## Large: External Funding

When you've proven your model, external funding can help you scale.

### Grants
Many programs fund community finance projects:

**Climate/Regeneration:**
- ReFi DAO Local Node grants [2]
- Gitcoin grants [3]
- Climate Collective funding

**Community Development:**
- Local community foundations
- Credit union community development funds
- Municipal grants

**Web3/Ecosystem:**
- Protocol grants
- Foundation funding
- Crowdfunding

How to get grants:

1. **Start micro**
   - Small grants ($500-5000) are easier to get
   - Build track record
   - Learn the process

2. **Document everything**
   - What you do
   - Who benefits
   - What you've learned

3. **Build relationships**
   - Talk to funders before applying
   - Go to events
   - Show you're for real

4. **Report honestly**
   - Even when things go wrong
   - Funders respect honesty
   - Builds trust

### Impact Investing
If your node will generate returns:

- Community investment rounds
- Regenerative investment funds
- DAOF-style funding

This is for more advanced nodes. It brings complexity.

## What to Avoid

### Don't Take Big Money Too Early

VC-style funding comes with strings. It can:
- Pressure fast growth
- Require giving up control
- Change your mission
- Attract people who want returns, not community

Take external money only when you're stable and ready.

### Don't Over-Fund

More money = more complexity:
- More reporting
- More expectations
- More stress

Start as simple as possible. Add money when you need it.

### Don't Forget Sustainability

How will you fund this in Year 2? Year 5?

Questions to ask:
- What happens if grants end?
- Can we generate local revenue?
- What's our backup plan?

### Don't Let Money Rule

Money is a tool, not the goal. Don't let funding decisions override community decisions.

---

## The Community Finance Principle

Here's the key insight: **your funding model shapes your community.**

| Funding Model | Community Effect |
|---------------|------------------|
| Member fees | High ownership, limits access |
| Grants | Growth but funder priorities |
| Revenue | Sustainability but business focus |
| Sweat equity | High participation, burnout risk |

Choose based on what kind of community you want.

Not all funding is equal. The source of your money affects what you can do with it.

---

## Practical Steps

Here's a realistic path:

### Month 1-3: Start Free
- Prove the concept
- Use free tools
- Learn what you actually need

### Month 4-6: Add Member Contributions  
- Small fees from committed members
- See what works

### Month 7-12: Formalize
- Set up proper structure if needed
- Build recurring revenue

### Year 2+: Seek External Funding
- Apply for grants
- Consider larger investments
- Scale what works

Don't jump to the end before you've proven the beginning.

---

## Funding Models and Governance

Different funding sources affect who controls your node:

- **Member-funded:** Members control
- **Grant-funded:** Funder has influence
- **Revenue-funded:** Customers have influence
- **Investor-funded:** Investors have influence

Think about who you want making decisions.

---

## Questions to Discuss

Before choosing funding:

1. What do we actually need money for?
2. How much is enough?
3. Who will provide it?
4. What do they expect in return?
5. What happens if money stops?

Be honest. Have the uncomfortable conversations early.
