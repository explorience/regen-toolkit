# Inflation Models for Community Currencies

*How to manage currency value over time*

---

## What Is Inflation?

Inflation is when a currency loses purchasing power over time. More currency exists chasing the same goods.

For community currencies, this can happen if:
- Too much currency is created relative to goods/services available
- Currency is hoarded and not circulated

---

## Inflation Models

### 1. No Inflation (Deflationary)

Currency has fixed supply or is removed from circulation over time.

**When it works**: For currencies designed as stores of value

**When it doesn't**: Discourages spending, can paralyze exchange

### 2. Managed Inflation

Currency supply grows at a predetermined rate (e.g., 2-5% annually).

**When it works**: Similar to national central banks

**When it doesn't**: Requires governance to set rates, can be political

### 3. Expiration Model

Currency units expire after a certain period.

**When it works**: Encourages circulation, prevents hoarding

**When it doesn't**: Discourages saving, can feel unfair

### 4. Community Fund Model

A portion of currency goes to community projects rather than individual earning.

**When it works**: Balances individual earning with community benefit

**When it doesn't**: Requires governance on fund allocation

---

## Choosing a Model

For most community currencies, a combination works best:
- Member-driven creation (earn currency through contribution)
- Community fund (some goes to collective projects)
- Optional expiration (encourages circulation)

## Moving Forward

Start simple. Adjust based on what you observe.
