# Token Supply and Community Currencies

*How to determine and manage your currency's total supply*

---

## The Supply Question

Every currency faces a fundamental question: how much of it should exist?

Too much, and it becomes worthless (hyperinflation). Too little, and there's not enough to facilitate exchange (deflationary pressure).

For community currencies, the answer is different than for national currencies or crypto tokens. You're not trying to optimize for economic growth — you're trying to facilitate exchange within your community.

---

## Supply Models

### Fixed Supply

The currency has a set maximum supply. No more will ever be created.

**Example**: Bitcoin (21 million cap)

**Pros**: Predictable, no inflation, clear scarcity

**Cons**: Can become too valuable to spend (hoarding), not responsive to community growth

**Best for**: Long-term stores of value, not active exchange mediums

### Elastic Supply

The supply expands or contracts based on rules.

**Pros**: Can respond to demand, prevents hoarding

**Cons**: Complex, can create uncertainty

**Best for**: Systems that need to maintain stable value

### Member-Driven Supply

New currency is created when members earn it through contribution.

**Pros**: Directly tied to productive activity, democratic

**Cons**: Requires governance decisions about what counts as contribution

**Best for**: Community timebanks and local exchange systems

---

## Determining the Right Supply

For community currencies, a practical approach:

1. **Start small**: Launch with enough for initial transactions
2. **Let need drive creation**: New currency enters when useful work is done
3. **Cap growth rate**: Limit max new currency per month/year to prevent inflation

**Key insight**: Community currency isn't an investment. It's a tool for exchange. Supply should facilitate exchange, not maximize value.

---

## Inflation Considerations

Inflation in community currencies: when more currency is created than the community needs, each unit becomes worth less.

**Managing inflation**:
- Link issuance to actual economic activity
- Have expiration dates on currency (use it or lose it)
- Allocate some to community projects rather than individual earning
- Monitor velocity (how fast currency changes hands)

---

## Moving Forward

Start with member-driven supply tied to contribution. Let the community decide as you grow.
