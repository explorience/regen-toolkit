# Hosting Your First Event

*A step-by-step guide to running your first local node gathering*

---

## The Most Important Thing

Just do it. Don't over-plan. Don't wait for perfect. Your first event will be messy, and that's okay.

---

## Before the Event

### Set a Date and Time
- Pick a date 2-4 weeks out
- Consider weekends or evenings
- Check for conflicts

### Find a Space
- Your home
- Library meeting room
- Cafe back section
- Co-working space
- Community center

### Set a Topic
- Something you're excited about
- Something relevant to your community
- Leave room for conversation

### Invite People
- Friends who might be interested
- Online community members
- Local meetup groups

---

## Day Of

### Set Up Early
- Arrive 30 minutes early
- Arrange seating for conversation
- Test any tech
- Put out sign-in sheet

### Welcome People
- Greet at door
- Make introductions
- Offer drinks/snacks

### Start Simple
- Brief welcome
- Introduce yourself
- Explain purpose
- Open for discussion

### End with Next Steps
- When's next event?
- How can people help?
- What's the communication channel?

---

## After the Event

### Thank People
- In the moment
- Follow up personally

### Follow Up
- Send summary
- Share resources
- Invite to next event

### Reflect
- What worked?
- What didn't?
- What to change?

---

## Common Mistakes

1. **Over-planning** - Plan enough, then launch
2. **Perfectionism** - Events don't need to be perfect
3. **Going Alone** - Get help
4. **No Follow Up** - The relationship continues after
