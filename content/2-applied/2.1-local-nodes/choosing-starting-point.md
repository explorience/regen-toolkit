# Choosing a Starting Point

*Guide to choosing a starting point*

---

## How to pick the right first project for your local node.

## The Paradox of Choice
There are dozens of things a local node could do: timebank, local currency, community treasury, events, mutual aid, governance experiments. Trying to do everything at once is how projects die.

## Selection Criteria

### 1. What Does Your Community Need?
Not what's trendy. What does your specific community actually need? Talk to people. Listen.

### 2. What Can You Sustain?
Be honest about capacity. A weekly event requires different resources than a monthly newsletter. Start with what your team can sustain for 6 months.

### 3. What Builds Relationships?
The best first project is one that brings people together and builds trust. Events and meals work better than governance frameworks as a starting point.

### 4. What Has a Quick Win?
Early success builds momentum. Choose something where you can show results in 4-8 weeks.

## Good Starting Points
1. **Monthly community meal** — lowest effort, highest relationship-building
2. **Skill share event** — demonstrates value exchange without money
3. **Simple timebank** — introduces community currency concepts
4. **Book/learning circle** — builds shared vocabulary
5. **Community fund** — even $500 pooled creates governance practice

## Bad Starting Points
1. **Full DAO deployment** — too complex too early
2. **Token launch** — solves a problem you don't have yet
3. **Platform building** — use existing tools first
4. **Governance framework** — govern what? Start doing, then govern

## Moving Forward
Pick one thing. Do it well. Learn from it. Then pick the next thing.
