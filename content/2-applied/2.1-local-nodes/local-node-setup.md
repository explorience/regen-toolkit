# Setting Up a Local Node

*Step-by-step guide to launching your own local node*

---

## Prerequisites

Before launching, you need:
- 5-7 committed people (see "Is Your Community Ready?")
- A shared purpose — why does this node exist?
- Basic communication infrastructure (group chat, email list)
- Willingness to experiment and iterate

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)

### Define Your Purpose
Write a one-paragraph mission statement together. Not aspirational fluff — concrete purpose.

Example: "GreenPill London exists to coordinate regenerative projects in London, manage a shared community treasury, and connect local builders with the global regen ecosystem."

### Establish Communication
- **Primary channel**: Signal, Telegram, or Discord group
- **Calendar**: Shared calendar for events
- **Documentation**: Notion, Google Docs, or a wiki for shared knowledge

### Hold Your First Gathering
A simple meal or meetup where founding members:
- Share why they're here
- Discuss the mission
- Agree on initial governance (how will we make decisions?)
- Set next steps

### Set Up Basic Infrastructure
- **Multisig wallet**: Gnosis Safe with 3-of-5 signing
- **Treasury seed**: Initial contributions (even $50 each)
- **Social presence**: Twitter/X account, website or landing page

---

## Phase 2: Launch (Month 3-4)

### Hold Regular Events
At least monthly. Mix of:
- Social gatherings (meals, coffee)
- Educational workshops
- Working sessions

### Onboard First Members
Use the buddy system. Each new member gets a personal welcome and orientation.

### Make Your First Collective Decision
Allocate a small amount from treasury to something. The decision matters less than the process. Practice governance early.

### Connect to Broader Network
Join Greenpill Network, ReFi DAO, or another relevant network. Attend their calls. Share your progress.

---

## Phase 3: Growth (Month 5-12)

### Expand Membership
Target 15-30 active members. Grow through:
- Events open to the public
- Word of mouth from existing members
- Social media presence
- Partnerships with local organizations

### Formalize Governance
Document how decisions are made. Consider:
- Consent-based process for policy decisions
- Quadratic voting for treasury allocation
- Working groups with delegated authority

### Launch Community Finance
If ready:
- Timebank for skill exchange
- Community fund for local projects
- Grant applications to ecosystem funds

### Measure Impact
Track what you're achieving:
- Events held
- Members active
- Treasury managed
- Projects funded
- Relationships built

---

## Common Mistakes

1. **Launching too fast**: Build relationships before launching infrastructure
2. **Too much tech**: Start simple, add tools as needed
3. **No regular cadence**: Consistency beats intensity
4. **Founder dependency**: Distribute leadership from the start
5. **Ignoring fun**: People join for purpose but stay for connection

---

## Moving Forward

Start now. Don't wait for perfect conditions. The best local nodes started with a few people, a meal, and a conversation about what's possible.
