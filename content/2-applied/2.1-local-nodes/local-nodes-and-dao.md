# Local Nodes and DAOs

*Guide to local nodes and daos*

---

## How local nodes relate to DAO structures, and when to formalize as a DAO.

## The Relationship

A local node isn't automatically a DAO. A DAO is a specific organizational form with on-chain governance. A local node is a community concept. Some local nodes become DAOs. Others stay informal. Both are valid.

## When to Stay Informal
- Small group (under 15)
- Low financial activity
- High trust among members
- Governance needs are simple

## When to Become a DAO
- Managing significant treasury
- Need transparent, verifiable governance
- Want to connect to broader DAO ecosystem
- Membership is growing beyond personal trust

## Hybrid Approaches
Many nodes use a hybrid: informal for social activities, DAO structure for financial decisions. This gives you the warmth of community with the accountability of on-chain governance.

## DAO Tools for Local Nodes
- **Snapshot**: Off-chain voting (free, easy)
- **Tally**: On-chain governance
- **Gnosis Safe**: Multisig treasury
- **Coordinape**: Contribution tracking
- **Hats Protocol**: Role management

## Moving Forward
Start informal. Add DAO tools as needed. Don't build governance infrastructure before you need it.
