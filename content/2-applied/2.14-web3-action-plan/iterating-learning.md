# Iterating and Learning

*Guide to iterating and learning*

---

## How to build a culture of continuous improvement in your local node.

## The Iteration Mindset
No plan survives first contact with reality. The communities that succeed are the ones that iterate quickly: try something, observe what happens, learn from it, and adjust.

## The Build-Measure-Learn Loop
1. **Build**: Try something small (an event, a process, a tool)
2. **Measure**: Track what happens (attendance, engagement, feedback)
3. **Learn**: What worked? What didn't? Why?
4. **Repeat**: Adjust and try again

## Retrospective Practice
After every significant activity:
- What went well?
- What didn't go well?
- What should we change?
- What should we keep?

Document these learnings. Share them with the community.

## Common Learning Traps
1. **Not documenting**: Lessons that aren't written down are forgotten
2. **Changing too much at once**: Change one variable at a time
3. **Ignoring negative feedback**: The uncomfortable feedback is often the most valuable
4. **Perfectionism**: "We need to get it right before launching" — no, launch and iterate
5. **Not sharing failures**: Other nodes can learn from your mistakes

## Moving Forward
Build iteration into your culture. Schedule retrospectives. Document learnings. Share openly.
