# Finding Support and Mentorship

*Guide to finding support and mentorship*

---

## How to find mentors, allies, and support networks for your local node.

## Why You Need Support
Building a local node is hard. Having allies, mentors, and peers makes it significantly more likely to succeed.

## Types of Support

### Mentorship
Experienced community builders who can guide you. Look for:
- Other node leaders in the Greenpill Network
- Cooperative development organizations
- Community foundation staff
- Social enterprise mentors

### Peer Networks
Other nodes at similar stages. You're facing the same challenges — learn together.

### Technical Support
People who can help with Web3 tools:
- Developer communities (ETHGlobal, local hackathon groups)
- Web3 community managers
- Protocol teams (many offer community support)

### Funding Support
Organizations that fund community projects:
- Gitcoin Grants
- Giveth
- Local community foundations
- Cooperative development funds

## Finding Mentors
1. Attend network events (Greenpill, ReFi, ETH conferences)
2. Reach out directly (most people are happy to help)
3. Join online communities (Discord servers, Telegram groups)
4. Ask for introductions from existing contacts

## Being a Good Mentee
- Come with specific questions, not vague asks
- Respect their time
- Follow through on advice (or explain why you didn't)
- Pay it forward — mentor others as you grow

## Moving Forward
Reach out. The Web3 community is remarkably generous with time and knowledge. Ask for help.
