# Impact Reporting Frameworks

*Guide to impact reporting frameworks*

---

## How to report your impact in ways that build trust and attract support.

## Why Report?
Reporting serves multiple audiences:
- **Members**: Shows their participation matters
- **Funders**: Demonstrates accountability
- **Partners**: Builds credibility
- **Public**: Attracts new members and support

## Report Types

### Monthly Update (Internal)
Quick summary for members:
- Key metrics this month
- Highlights and wins
- Challenges and learnings
- Next month's priorities

**Format**: 1-page email or message. Takes 30 minutes to create.

### Quarterly Report (Members + Partners)
Deeper analysis:
- Trend data (3-month view)
- Stories of impact
- Financial transparency
- Strategic reflections

**Format**: 2-4 pages. Takes 2-3 hours to create.

### Annual Report (Public)
Comprehensive review:
- Full year metrics
- Impact stories
- Financial statements
- Strategic plan for next year

**Format**: 10-20 pages. Takes 1-2 weeks to create.

## Best Practices
1. **Be honest**: Report failures alongside successes
2. **Tell stories**: Numbers need narratives
3. **Be consistent**: Same format, same schedule
4. **Make it visual**: Charts, infographics, photos
5. **Share widely**: Don't just file it — distribute it

## Common Mistakes
1. Only reporting when things go well
2. Too many numbers, not enough stories
3. Inconsistent reporting schedule
4. Reports no one reads (too long, too dense)

## Moving Forward
Start with monthly internal updates. Add quarterly reports when you have partners. Annual reports when you have funders.
