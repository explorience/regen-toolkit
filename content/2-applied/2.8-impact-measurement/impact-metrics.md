# Impact Metrics for Community Finance

*Guide to impact metrics for community finance*

---

## What to measure, how to measure it, and why it matters for your local node.

## Why Measure Impact?
Without measurement, you're guessing. Impact metrics help you:
- Know if you're achieving your mission
- Communicate value to members and funders
- Identify what's working and what isn't
- Build credibility with external partners

## Types of Metrics

### Output Metrics (What You Did)
- Number of events held
- Members participating
- Transactions completed
- Treasury balance
- Projects funded

### Outcome Metrics (What Changed)
- Skills developed
- Relationships formed
- Economic value exchanged
- Community resilience indicators
- Member satisfaction scores

### Impact Metrics (Why It Matters)
- Local economic multiplier effect
- Reduction in financial exclusion
- Environmental outcomes
- Social cohesion indicators
- Policy influence

## Choosing Your Metrics

### The 5 Essential Metrics
Every local node should track at minimum:
1. **Active members** (participated in last 30 days)
2. **Events/activities** (number and attendance)
3. **Value exchanged** (in local currency, timebank hours, or fiat)
4. **Member satisfaction** (quarterly survey)
5. **Treasury health** (balance and runway)

### Advanced Metrics
As you grow:
- Network analysis (who connects to whom)
- Community currency velocity
- Governance participation rates
- External partnerships formed
- Stories of change (qualitative)

## Tools for Measurement
- Spreadsheets (simplest, always works)
- Dune Analytics (for on-chain data)
- Typeform/Google Forms (for surveys)
- Custom dashboards (for advanced tracking)

## Moving Forward
Start measuring from day one, even if it's just a simple spreadsheet. Data you don't collect is data you can't use.
